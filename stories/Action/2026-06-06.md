# The Urgent Lunch

**Genre:** Action | **Date:** 2026-06-06

### Characters
*   **Leo:** A fast and street-smart bicycle messenger.
*   **Mr. Thorne:** A calm and watchful client.
*   **The 'Tail':** Two serious-looking men in a dark van.

### The Story
Leo sat on his bike, chewing a piece of gum. His phone rang. It was a new number. "Leo, this is Thorne," a deep voice said. "I have an urgent job. Highest pay. Pick up at Elm Street warehouse, drop off at Pine Tower, room seven. You have exactly forty-five minutes. Do not be late. Do not stop. This is vital."

Leo quickly rode to the warehouse. A man, Mr. Thorne, stood by a big truck. He handed Leo a plain, metal lunchbox. It had a small lock on it. "This box must not be opened. Its contents are secret. Get it there safe and on time. Your life, and more, depends on it." Thorne's face was serious. Leo felt a cold shiver. What was in this lunchbox?

He strapped the box to his bag and pedaled hard. As he turned onto Main Street, he saw a dark van pull out from a side road. It started following him. Leo sped up. The van kept pace. This wasn't a normal delivery. He cut down a narrow alley, kicking off a trash can to block the way. The van hit the can with a loud crash, but kept coming.

Leo weaved through market stalls, dodging people and carts. He heard angry shouts behind him. The van was close. He took a sharp turn into a park, riding fast on the dirt path. The van could not follow there. He thought he lost them. He caught his breath for a second. But as he came out of the park, the van was waiting. The two men inside looked tough. They wanted the box.

Fear mixed with a rush of adrenaline. He knew these streets like the back of his hand. He took a shortcut through a building's lobby, startling office workers. He jumped over a low wall, almost crashing. The van couldn't follow everywhere. He saw Pine Tower in the distance. Not much time left. He pushed harder, his legs burning. The van was still there, a constant threat. He had to get there. He had to deliver the box.

Finally, he reached Pine Tower. He saw the van screech to a stop behind him. No time for the elevator. He dashed inside and ran up the stairs, two steps at a time. Eighteen floors felt like a hundred. He burst into room seven, breathless, sweat dripping from his face. The clock on the wall showed one minute to go.

### Conclusion
Mr. Thorne sat at a desk, looking calm. The two men from the van stood next to him, no longer looking tough. They were smiling faintly. Mr. Thorne reached out and took the lunchbox from Leo. "Right on time, Leo. And the box is in perfect condition. Excellent work." He unlocked the box and lifted the lid. Inside was a neatly wrapped sandwich and an apple. "This," Mr. Thorne said, picking up the sandwich, "is my lunch. And this whole chase? That was your final interview for a new job. We needed someone with your speed, your cool head, and your ability to follow orders under pressure. The van, the urgency – all part of the test. You passed. Can you start Monday?"
