# Mildred's Melody

**Genre:** Action | **Date:** 2026-04-25

### Characters
*   Leo: A careful accountant, usually focused on numbers and routine.
*   Aunt Mildred: Leo's elderly aunt, who has a surprising hidden past.
*   Two Guys: Large, determined men trying to steal the box.

### The Story
Leo liked things orderly. Numbers in neat columns, bills paid on time. His Saturday was set: groceries, then quiet reading. But then his phone rang. Aunt Mildred.
"Leo, dear boy! A tiny favor," her voice chirped. "Could you pick up my special cake from 'The Sweet Spot' bakery? It's for my collection."
Leo sighed. Aunt Mildred collected many odd things, never actual cakes. But he always helped her.
At 'The Sweet Spot,' the baker, a wide man named Frank, handed Leo a plain brown box. It was heavy. Too heavy for cake. "Special order," Frank grunted, avoiding eye contact.
"It feels like a brick," Leo said.
Frank just gave a small, nervous smile. "Yep. Very special."
Leo shrugged. Aunt Mildred was strange. He walked out onto the busy street, the heavy box tucked under his arm. He pictured his quiet afternoon.

Suddenly, a dark van stopped fast nearby. Two large men jumped out. They wore dark clothes and serious faces. One pointed at Leo.
"The box!" he yelled.
Leo froze. He thought they meant someone else. But they ran straight at him. Instinct took over. He clutched the heavy box tighter and ran.
He wasn't a runner. His lungs burned. The men were fast. He dodged a lamppost, then a woman with a stroller. He heard them shouting behind him.
He ducked into a fruit market. Oranges tumbled from a stall as he crashed past. The men were right on his heels. He shoved a loose cart, sending it rolling. It slowed them for a second.

Leo burst out of the market and onto a main road. A city bus was just pulling away from the stop. He sprinted, jumped, and squeezed through the closing doors. Gasping, he looked back. The two men were stuck on the sidewalk, looking angry.
Leo slumped onto a seat, heart pounding. The bus smelled of old air and cheap cleaner. He gripped the box. What was in it? And why did these men want it?
He got off a few stops later, near Aunt Mildred's quiet street. He walked quickly, looking over his shoulder. The street was empty. Good.
He reached Aunt Mildred's old, flowery house and fumbled with the key. He burst inside, slamming the door shut.

### Conclusion
Aunt Mildred sat calmly on her sofa, sipping tea. "My goodness, Leo. You look like you've seen a ghost. And your shirt is torn."
Leo put the heavy box on her antique coffee table. "Aunt Mildred, two men tried to steal this! They chased me all over the city!"
She smiled gently. "Did they now? Well, you did wonderfully." She reached for the box.
Leo watched as she opened it. Inside, nestled on velvet, was not a cake, but an old, silver music box. It had tiny, complex gears visible under a glass lid.
Mildred wound a small key on the side. A sweet, clear melody filled the room.
"This," she said, looking at Leo with a glint in her eye he'd never seen before, "is not just a music box, dear. It's a key. And some people are always trying to get their hands on what doesn't belong to them." She paused, then added, "You have good instincts, Leo. Better than I thought a simple numbers man would have." Leo stared at his aunt, the music playing softly, realizing his quiet, orderly world had just been turned upside down.
