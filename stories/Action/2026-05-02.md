# The Mittens Delivery

**Genre:** Action | **Date:** 2026-05-02

### Characters
Leo: A veteran delivery driver, knows the city's every street and alley.
Mittens: A fluffy, very special cat.
Mrs. Gable: An elderly woman, Mittens' owner.
Elara: Leader of the 'Paw Protectors,' a dedicated animal welfare group.

### The Story
Leo’s beat-up old van smelled of coffee and stale pretzels. He’d driven this city for twenty years, delivering everything from hot pizzas to heavy factory parts. But today’s job was different. The payment offer was huge, enough to fix his van's dodgy brakes and maybe even get a new stereo. The instructions were simple: pick up a small, locked metal box from a specific address, then deliver it across town. No questions.

He arrived at the first spot, a quiet house on Elm Street. A man in a dark suit met him at the door, handed him the box. It was light, and Leo felt a soft vibration inside. Then, a low rumble, a purr. Leo frowned. A cat? He looked at the man, who just nodded, his face unreadable. "Deliver it safely. No stops. Do not open the box."

Leo put the box on the passenger seat, the purring a gentle rhythm. As he pulled away from the curb, a black sedan, clean and new, shot out from a side street and sped up behind him. Not a cop car. The hairs on Leo’s neck stood up. This wasn’t normal.

He turned a corner, then another, trying to lose the sedan. It stuck to his tail like glue. Leo knew these streets better than anyone. He swerved into a narrow alleyway, scraping paint off his van’s side mirror. The sedan was too wide to follow. But a fast, quiet motorbike roared past his alley exit, blocking his way. Two people, in black gear, jumped off.

"Drop the package!" one shouted.

Leo didn't drop packages. Not ever. He slammed the van into reverse, bumping the motorbike and sending it clattering. He then sped forward, tires squealing, leaving the surprised riders in the alley dust. He took a route he hadn’t used in years, a maze of back roads that wound through the old market district. Shoppers scattered as he drove carefully but quickly between stalls piled with fresh fruit and colorful fabrics. The black sedan appeared again, cutting through a main road, trying to intercept him.

This was crazy. For a cat?

Leo saw his chance. A construction site lay ahead, usually blocked off. But today, the chain link fence had a small gap. He didn't hesitate, crashing through it. Dust and grit flew. He bounced over dirt piles, weaving between concrete mixers and stacks of lumber. The sedan tried to follow but got stuck in thick mud. He saw the black-clad motorbike riders again, cutting across a pedestrian bridge, trying to head him off at the next intersection. They were relentless.

He hit the main road, the destination just a few blocks away. He could see the apartment building now. The motorbike riders pulled alongside his van, one of them gesturing fiercely for him to stop. Leo grit his teeth, pushing the van faster. He made a sharp turn into the apartment building’s parking lot, hitting the brakes hard. The motorbike overshot the entrance, spinning wildly.

Leo jumped out, the purring box clutched tight. He ran for the building entrance, the motorbike riders now racing after him on foot. He burst through the lobby doors, hitting the elevator button again and again. The doors slid open. He leaped inside, pressing the button for the top floor. Just as the doors closed, he saw the riders, faces determined, rushing towards the elevator.

The ride up felt endless. The box was still purring softly against his chest. What kind of cat was this? A secret agent cat?

The elevator chimed, stopping on the penthouse floor. He hurried down the quiet hall to apartment 12B. He knocked. The door opened slowly. An old woman, her hair white and her face kind, peered out. Her eyes were warm but held a hint of sadness.

"Leo? You're here." Her voice was soft.

He held out the box. "Delivery for Mrs. Gable."

She took the box, her hands trembling slightly. "Oh, Mittens," she whispered, her eyes filling with tears. She carefully undid the latch. Inside, curled up on a soft blanket, was the most beautiful cat Leo had ever seen. It was fluffy, with bright blue eyes, and it stretched, letting out a soft meow. Mittens. The cat looked perfectly healthy, perfectly happy.

"Thank you, thank you, Leo," Mrs. Gable said, stroking the cat. "I was so worried. They said they were rescuing her."

### Conclusion
Just then, the elevator doors opened again. The two motorbike riders, now joined by a stern-looking woman, stepped out. They saw Mrs. Gable holding the cat. "There she is!" the woman declared, pointing at Mittens. "That poor creature needed our help! We knew you couldn't care for her properly, Mrs. Gable!" She looked at Leo. "You were just delaying her true freedom!"

Mrs. Gable looked confused. "Freedom? She was just at the groomer's. I couldn't pick her up myself because of my bad hip, so I paid extra for a special delivery. Mittens hates the car."

The stern woman, Elara, lowered her head. "A groomer? We... we thought she was being held against her will. A high-value feline, a purebred Himalayan, in a special locked box, being rushed across the city? We assumed the worst. We are The Paw Protectors. Our mission is to save every animal from perceived neglect." She then looked at the two riders behind her. "We tracked the wrong lead again, didn't we, boys?" The riders shifted uncomfortably.

Leo just stared, then back at his van, scraped and dented, and thought about the huge payment. All for Mittens, the purebred Himalayan cat who just had a fancy haircut. He suddenly found the whole mess incredibly funny.
