# The Granny Gauntlet

**Genre:** Action | **Date:** 2026-05-16

### Characters
*   Leo: A young, skilled bike courier.
*   Mr. Silas Thorne: Leo's client, an eager, slightly frantic businessman.
*   Granny Willow: The recipient, an elderly woman with a playful glint in her eyes.
*   The Drivers: Men in black cars, Granny Willow's competitive relatives.

### The Story
Leo checked his watch. Another slow afternoon. Then his phone buzzed. A new job, top dollar. The client, Mr. Silas Thorne, sounded stressed. "A small wooden box," Mr. Thorne said, "Deliver it to this address, no stops, no looking back. It's... important." Leo nodded, though the man couldn't see him. He picked up the plain, light wooden box. It felt empty, light as a feather. He tucked it safely in his insulated delivery bag and jumped on his bike.

The moment Leo pulled out of the alley, a black sedan peeled from the curb. Too fast, too close. Leo knew the drill. Not a customer in a hurry. Someone was after the box. He kicked his bike into a higher gear, weaving through traffic. The sedan tried to cut him off, but Leo was quicker. He shot down a narrow lane meant for walkers, leaving the car stuck behind a parked truck. His heart thumped hard.

He thought he'd lost them. But at the next turn, a black van roared into view. They had multiple cars. Leo cursed under his breath. He darted into a bustling outdoor market, a blur of fruit stalls and shouting vendors. A man jumped out of the van, trying to grab him. Leo swerved, sending a pyramid of oranges crashing down. The man slipped. Leo’s bike skidded on a wet patch, almost sending him flying. He kept his grip, righted the bike, and burst out the other side of the market. His leg ached from the near fall.

The chase went on. Through city parks, across busy bridges. Leo felt the wind whipping past, his breath ragged. He glanced back. They were still there, always a car or two. He took a risky jump down a flight of stairs, landing hard. His bike groaned, but held. He saw his destination ahead: a quiet street, old houses with neat gardens. He was almost there. He pushed harder, every muscle burning.

The black cars screeched to a halt behind him as he pulled up to the target house. It was an old place, brick, with roses climbing the porch. He didn't wait. He leaped off his bike, ran up the steps, and hit the doorbell. The cars were emptying now, men in dark clothes walking towards the porch. Leo felt a cold sweat. He held the small wooden box tight.

### Conclusion
The door opened slowly. An old woman with kind eyes and a warm smile stood there. "Hello, dear," she said, her voice soft. Leo, panting, held out the box. "Delivery for... Granny Willow?" The men from the cars were now on the lawn, watching. She took the box. "Oh, wonderful! You're much faster than my nephew, Silas. He usually tries to get it here himself. This year he hired you, did he?" She winked. The lead man from the black cars stepped forward. "Grandma," he sighed, "Silas is cheating again! We almost had him this time." Granny Willow chuckled, "Better luck next year, boys! My annual 'Treasure Transit' game is always more fun with a bit of a chase." She opened the box. Inside was a single, perfectly ripe red apple. "Perfect for my pie!" Leo stared, mouth open, as the 'bad guys' grumbled good-naturedly, shaking their heads. "What was that for?" he asked, completely lost. Granny Willow just smiled. "A bit of family tradition, dear. And a very exciting way to get my groceries."
