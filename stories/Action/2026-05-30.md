# The Final Slice

**Genre:** Action | **Date:** 2026-05-30

### Characters
Lena: A skilled baker, delivering a vital wedding cake.
Mick: Lena's assistant, on the phone from the bakery.
Sarah: The frantic wedding planner, chasing Lena.
Mark: The groom's brother, also chasing Lena.

### The Story
Lena gripped the steering wheel, her knuckles white. The city streets blurred. Her old delivery van, usually slow, was now screaming its way through traffic. On the passenger seat, a huge, five-tier wedding cake swayed gently in its box. It was late. Very late. This was her best friend's wedding cake, and it had to be perfect. She took a corner too fast, and the cake box slid an inch. Her heart jumped.

Her phone buzzed. It was Mick, her assistant. "Lena! Where are you? Sarah from the wedding is losing it!"
"Tell her I'm almost there!" Lena shouted back, swerving to avoid a bus. "Just a few more blocks!"

Suddenly, a black sedan shot out from a side street, cutting her off. Lena slammed the brakes. The cake lurched forward. "Hey!" she yelled, leaning on the horn. The sedan didn't move. Then, a dark blue van pulled up behind her, blocking her escape. Lena’s blood ran cold. This wasn't a normal traffic stop.

"Mick, something's wrong," she mumbled into the phone. "I think... I think they're trying to get the cake!"
"The cake? Why would anyone steal a cake?" Mick sounded confused.
"I don't know! But they look serious!"

Lena spotted a narrow alleyway. She floored the gas, scraping the side of her van as she squeezed through. The black sedan cursed and reversed. The blue van was faster, swerving expertly to follow her. Lena checked her mirror. A man was yelling from the van's window, waving his arms like a madman. He didn't look like a typical thief; he looked like someone having a very bad day.

She weaved through traffic, dodging taxis and other delivery trucks. The blue van stayed tight on her tail. Lena remembered the hours she’d spent baking this cake, the careful layers, the delicate icing. It wasn’t just flour and sugar; it was a promise to her best friend. She wasn't letting anyone take it.

She took a sharp turn into a busy market street, scattering a few fruit stalls. Apologies could wait. The blue van had to slow down to navigate the tight space. Lena spotted an open parking spot near the wedding venue. She punched the gas, slid her van into the space, and killed the engine. The cake, against all odds, seemed okay.

The blue van screeched to a halt right behind her. Lena grabbed a tire iron from under her seat. She took a deep breath. She wasn't a fighter, but she was ready. The van doors burst open.

### Conclusion
Out stepped a woman in a fancy dress, her face red and streaked with tears. It was Sarah, the wedding planner. Behind her, a younger man, sweating and out of breath. He looked familiar. Mark, the groom's brother. They weren’t holding weapons. They looked frantic.

"Lena!" Sarah cried, rushing forward. "Thank goodness we caught you! You were going so fast!"
Lena held up the tire iron. "Caught me? You chased me! You tried to steal the cake!"
Mark put his hands up, panting. "Steal it? No! We were trying to stop you!" He pointed at the large cake box. "That's the wrong cake! We called your shop. Mick told us you accidentally swapped the boxes. The groom is allergic to nuts, and that cake has walnuts in every layer!"

Lena looked from the cake box to Mark, then to Sarah, whose face was now a mix of relief and exhaustion. Her tire iron slowly dropped to the ground. The high-speed chase, the fear, the desperate fight... all for a cake mix-up and a severe nut allergy. She stared at the beautiful cake, suddenly seeing the danger hidden within its sugary layers.
