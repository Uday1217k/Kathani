# The Quiet Borrower

**Genre:** Horror | **Date:** 2026-07-14

### Characters
Clara: A young woman living alone in an apartment.
Mrs. Gable: Clara's elderly neighbor, who lives downstairs.

### The Story
Clara liked her small apartment. It was neat and quiet. Every morning, she made coffee and read the news on her phone. One Tuesday, she went to get her favorite mug, the blue one with a chip. It was not on the drying rack where she left it. It was in the cupboard, next to the plates. Clara frowned. She always left it out. She just shrugged. Maybe she put it away and forgot.

The next week, something else happened. Clara kept her books in a certain order on her shelf. History books on the top, stories on the middle, cooking books on the bottom. She knew her system. But one evening, her big cookbook, ‘Simple Meals,’ was on the middle shelf, next to her mystery novels. She took it down, put it back. This was strange. She never moved it.

A few days later, she noticed her houseplant, a small green fern, had a tiny red ribbon tied around one leaf. Clara did not own red ribbons. She took it off, feeling a small chill. This was not a big deal, just a ribbon. But it meant someone had been in her home. Or was she going crazy?

Clara started to check things. She put a small hair on her bedroom doorframe, low down, where it would be hard to see. She put a specific pencil under her living room rug, just a tiny bit sticking out. She went to work, feeling jumpy. When she came home, the hair was gone. The pencil was pushed fully under the rug. Her heart beat fast. Someone was coming into her apartment. But why? Nothing was stolen. Her TV, her laptop, her wallet – all were there.

She got a small camera, one of those tiny ones. She hid it on a shelf, facing her living room door. She set it to record movement. The next morning, she checked the video. It showed nothing. No one came in. But when she looked around, her favorite blanket, folded neatly on her sofa, now had a new patch sewn onto it. A small, flowery patch she had never seen before. Her breath caught. This was not possible.

Clara felt truly scared now. She couldn't sleep well. She called the police, but they said without proof of theft or a break-in, there wasn't much they could do. She told them about the moving things, the patch, the ribbon. The officer just looked tired. Clara knew they thought she was imagining things.

She decided to stay home from work the next day. She would wait. She would hide. She would catch whoever it was. She sat very still in her bedroom, door slightly ajar. Hours passed. Nothing. She made herself a sandwich. Still nothing. She almost gave up.

Just as the sun began to set, she heard a faint click from her front door. It was so quiet, she almost missed it. Then, a soft creak. Clara held her breath. She peeked out. The front door was open just a crack. A hand reached in. It was an old, wrinkled hand. It held a small, painted ceramic bird. The hand gently placed the bird on her small entryway table, next to her keys. Clara knew that bird. It sat on Mrs. Gable's windowsill, downstairs.

Clara watched, frozen, as Mrs. Gable herself, her sweet, always-smiling downstairs neighbor, stepped quietly into the apartment. She didn't look like a thief. She wore her house dress and a kind smile. She hummed a soft tune. She walked to Clara's kitchen, picked up a sponge, and put it back in a different spot. She straightened a picture frame on the wall. Then, she walked over to Clara's little fern. She bent down, gently feeling the leaves.

### Conclusion
Mrs. Gable plucked a dead leaf from the fern, then smiled softly to herself. She turned, walking towards the door, just as Clara stumbled out of her bedroom. Mrs. Gable saw her. Her eyes widened for a second, then her smile returned. "Oh, Clara dear!" she chirped. "I just popped in to water your plant. And I noticed your spice rack looked a bit messy, so I tidied it for you. And I found that lovely blue mug of yours! I put it away for you. You know," she said, her voice dropping to a whisper, "my daughter always left things out too. So I know how it is. It's nice to have someone help, isn't it? Just like family." She winked, gave a little wave, and softly closed the door behind her, leaving Clara standing alone in her now perfectly "tidy" apartment, with a chill far colder than any ghost could bring.
