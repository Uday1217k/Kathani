# The Copper Earth

**Genre:** Horror | **Date:** 2026-04-28

### Characters
*   Liam: A young man, the main character.
*   Sarah: Liam's girlfriend.
*   Mark: Liam's colleague.
*   Mrs. Henderson: Liam's elderly neighbor.

### The Story
Liam first tasted it in his usual morning coffee. It wasn't bitter, not sour, but a faint, weird note. Like licking an old copper coin that had been dropped in damp soil. He frowned, took another sip. Still there. He checked the date on the coffee bag. Fine. He shrugged, put it down to a bad brew.

A few days later, he had it again. This time, in a slice of pizza from his favorite takeout place. The cheesy, spicy warmth was there, but beneath it, that same faint copper-earth taste. He picked at the crust, wondering if the oven was dirty. He asked Sarah, his girlfriend, to taste it. She took a bite. "Delicious," she said, her eyes bright. Liam tried again. The taste was clear to him. He started to worry about his health. Maybe a strange new allergy?

He went to the doctor. They ran some tests. Everything came back normal. "Probably just your taste buds playing tricks," the doctor said with a smile. Liam left feeling more confused than before. The taste wasn't going away. It was subtle, but always there, lurking under the real flavors of food.

He started to notice something else. The taste seemed stronger when he was around certain people. His colleague, Mark, at the office. Mark always ate silently at his desk. When Liam was near Mark's desk, eating his lunch, the copper-earth taste was sharp, almost metallic in his mouth. But when Mark left the room, the taste faded from Liam's food.

Liam started watching. He would grab a quick snack, then walk past different people. His heart thumped. When he passed Mrs. Henderson, the sweet lady who lived down the hall, offering him a homemade cookie, the taste in his mouth spiked. The cookie tasted normal at first, then that familiar, awful note flared up. But Mrs. Henderson's smile never changed. Her eyes were kind, but somehow, they seemed a little too fixed, like painted glass.

Panic grew in Liam. It wasn't the food. It wasn't him. It was *them*. Some of them. Not everyone. Sarah, for example, never made his food taste like that. His best friend, David, didn't either. But Mark and Mrs. Henderson did. And a few other people he saw around town. He started to avoid them, to cross the street, to make excuses at work.

He noticed other things about the "copper-earth people." They often blinked a beat too late. Their jokes fell flat, their laughter sounded just a tiny bit forced. Their movements were too smooth, like puppets on invisible strings. They were still there, still talking, still living their lives, but something inside them was… hollow. Replaced. The taste was the only way he knew.

One evening, Sarah cooked his favorite lasagna. The house smelled wonderful. Liam felt a wave of relief. No copper-earth taste here. He took a forkful, chewing slowly. It was perfect. Just cheesy, tomato goodness. He looked at Sarah across the table, her face soft in the lamplight. She smiled. A real smile. He took another bite.

Then, as he swallowed, he felt a faint, familiar tingle on his tongue. He paused, his fork halfway to his mouth. No. It couldn't be. The taste was very faint, almost gone, but it was there. That unmistakable, metallic, damp earth taste. It wasn't coming from the lasagna, which still tasted fine. It wasn't coming from Sarah, whose presence still felt warm and real. It was coming from… inside his own mouth. A tiny, spreading patch of nothing, tasting of old copper and cold soil.

### Conclusion
Liam dropped his fork. He tried to speak, but his tongue felt heavy, and the taste bloomed, stronger now, like a flower of rust and cold earth inside his very being. He stared at Sarah, and in her sweet, questioning eyes, he saw not a reflection of his fear, but a flicker of that same too-smooth, too-fixed gaze he had learned to dread. The taste wasn't just a warning anymore. It was a part of him.
