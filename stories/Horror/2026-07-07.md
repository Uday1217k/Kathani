# The Perpetual Lawn

**Genre:** Horror | **Date:** 2026-07-07

### Characters
*   Arthur: An older man, meticulous and proud of his garden.
*   Eleanor: Arthur's wife, patient and practical.

### The Story
Arthur loved his lawn. It was a neat, green carpet, the envy of the street. Every Saturday, rain or shine, he'd start his old petrol mower and give it a perfect trim. He’d spend hours, making sure every blade was just so. It was his pride.

One Saturday, Arthur did his usual chore. He cut the grass, swept the clippings, and put the mower away, feeling good. But Sunday morning, when he looked out, his heart sank. The lawn was long. Not just a little long, but thick and shaggy, like he hadn’t touched it in weeks.

He rubbed his eyes. No, it was definitely long. He walked out, felt the tall blades tickle his ankles. He frowned. Had he forgotten to mow? Impossible. He remembered the whir of the blades, the smell of fresh cut grass, the satisfied feeling.

He shrugged it off, put it down to an old man’s momentary lapse. Next Saturday, he mowed it again, even more carefully. He watched the blades fall. He even took a photo with his phone when he was done. Perfect.

Sunday morning, he peered out. The lawn was long again. Just as long, just as shaggy. His stomach churned. He looked at the photo on his phone. It showed a perfectly cut lawn. He looked out the window again. The long grass waved at him.

“Eleanor,” he called, his voice tight. “Come here, quick.”
Eleanor came to the window, sipping her tea. “What is it, dear?”
“The lawn!” Arthur pointed, his hand shaking slightly. “It’s grown back. Already!”
Eleanor looked out, then back at Arthur, a small smile playing on her lips. “Arthur, it looks perfectly fine. You did such a good job yesterday.”

Arthur stared. “No, Eleanor, look! It’s tall! It’s like a jungle out there!”
Eleanor walked closer to the window, peered out intently. “It’s lovely and short, Arthur. Maybe you need new glasses?” She chuckled softly.

Arthur felt a chill. He knew what he saw. He had felt the long grass. He had watched it from the window. He went outside, the grass still brushing against his shins. He picked up a blade, long and green. He brought it back inside to show Eleanor.

“See?” he said, holding it up.
Eleanor squinted. “See what, dear? It’s just a short bit of grass.” She looked at him with gentle concern.

Arthur felt like he was losing his mind. He spent the next week watching the lawn, peering through the window. He didn’t see it grow. But every Saturday morning, without fail, it was long again. And every time he mowed it, and every time he looked at it on Sunday, it was long again, only for *him*.

He stopped sleeping well. He paced the house, muttering about the grass. Eleanor tried to calm him, suggested he see a doctor. But Arthur knew. He wasn't crazy. The grass was playing a trick on him. Or something else was.

One Saturday, as he was about to start the mower, he saw something new. Deep within the long, green blades, a faint, metallic glint. He knelt down, pushing the grass aside. There, half-buried, was a small, silver tag. He pulled it out. It was a thin, worn piece of metal, like a dog tag, but too small. On it, etched in tiny letters, was a single word: "RESET."

Arthur stared at it, his mind racing. Reset? Reset what? The grass? His mind? His world?
He looked at his perfect, green lawn, the one that only he saw as long. He looked at his wife, who saw a normal, cut lawn. He looked at the tag.

He gripped the tag tightly in his hand. “Eleanor!” he called, his voice full of a strange mix of fear and excitement. “I found something!”

Eleanor came out, a worried look on her face. “Arthur, what is it now? You’re scaring me.”

Arthur held up the tiny metal tag. “Look! I found this in the grass. It says ‘RESET’.”

Eleanor peered at the tag in his hand. Her eyes widened slightly. Her face went pale. “Arthur,” she whispered, her voice barely audible. “Where did you find that?”

Arthur pointed. “Right there, in the long grass.”

### Conclusion
Eleanor took a deep, shaky breath. She looked at the tag, then at Arthur, a new, terrible understanding dawning in her eyes. “Arthur,” she said, her voice strained. “That’s *my* tag.”

Arthur frowned. “Your tag? What are you talking about?”

Eleanor reached into her pocket. She pulled out an identical, tiny silver tag. On it, etched in tiny letters, was a single word: "PAUSE."

Eleanor looked at Arthur, her eyes filled with sorrow. “We used to have such a beautiful garden,” she said softly, "before the illness took hold. You loved to mow it. Every day, you’d forget you had done it. Every day, you’d try to mow it again. It broke my heart. The doctors... they gave me these. To help you. To help us." She looked down at the "PAUSE" tag in her hand, then at the "RESET" tag in his. "I would press 'PAUSE' when you went to bed, so you could rest. And every morning, I would press 'RESET' to make you feel like it was a new day, and the lawn was fresh again for you to tend. I made you think the grass was growing fast, so you'd have a reason to do the thing you loved most." She met his gaze, tears welling in her eyes. "Oh, Arthur. You weren't seeing the grass grow. You were just starting your day all over again." The perfect, short lawn shimmered under the morning sun, waiting for its master to begin his endless, loving chore.
