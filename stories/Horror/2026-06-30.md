# The Threaded Mirror

**Genre:** Horror | **Date:** 2026-06-30

### Characters
Elara: A young woman living alone.

### The Story
Elara was a creature of habit. Every morning, after waking up, she went straight to the bathroom. First, she brushed her teeth. Then, she always looked in the mirror. Not for long, just a quick check of her face.

One Tuesday, as she rinsed her mouth, she saw something odd. A tiny grey thread, thin as a hair, seemed to be caught in her reflection's hair. She reached up to pluck it out. Her fingers met the cold, smooth glass. The thread wasn't in her hair. It was in the mirror itself, like a tiny scratch or a hair stuck *behind* the glass. It was almost invisible. She rubbed it with her thumb. Nothing happened. She shrugged. Maybe it was just a tiny flaw in the mirror.

The next morning, the thread was thicker. It was still grey, but now it looked more like a tiny, pale vein. It ran from the corner of her reflection's eye, down her cheek. It looked like it was under the skin, not on it. She touched the mirror. It was still smooth. She touched her own cheek. Her skin was smooth too. The vein-like line was only in the reflection. A cold shiver ran down her back.

Days passed. The grey line grew. It started to branch out, like a tiny spiderweb of delicate grey lines all over her reflection’s face. They spread across her neck, down to her hands when she held them up. When she moved, the threads moved too, like they were part of her reflection’s skin. They weren't cracks. They were *inside* the reflection. Elara tried to ignore the mirror, but she couldn't. Each morning, she was drawn to it. Each time, the threads were more complex, forming swirling patterns. They even had a faint, pulsing light now, like tiny, glowing nerves. Her reflection looked tired, its eyes sunken behind the glowing grey network.

One Saturday morning, Elara forced herself to look closely. The threads pulsed brightly, like a nest of tiny, living things. Her reflection’s eyes, dull and deep-set, met hers. Then, her reflection smiled. It was a wide, toothy grin that stretched the threads taut across its face. It was not Elara’s smile. Her reflection’s mouth opened wider, wider, until it was just a dark, empty hole in the middle of the glowing grey network. The threads around the hole began to *move*, twisting and tightening, pulling the reflected skin inward, making the hole bigger.

Elara screamed. She slammed her hands against the mirror. The threads in the reflection writhed for a moment, then went still. The hole remained, a gaping, black wound on her reflection's face.

### Conclusion
Elara stood there, breathing hard, staring at the horror in the glass. Slowly, cautiously, she raised a trembling hand to her own mouth. Her fingers met smooth skin, but as she pressed, she felt a slight give. A faint, almost imperceptible *crack*. A tiny, grey thread, just like the first one she saw in the mirror, was starting to bloom on her own skin, right at the corner of her lip. And from deep within her, a new, strange hunger began to bloom too. The mirror wasn't just showing her an image; it was showing her what she was becoming.
