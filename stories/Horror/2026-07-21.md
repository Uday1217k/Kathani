# The Collector

**Genre:** Horror | **Date:** 2026-07-21

### Characters
Clara: A young woman who lives alone in her small apartment.

### The Story
Clara liked things neat. Her small apartment was always tidy, every book on its shelf, every mug in its place. She lived by a schedule, a simple rhythm of work, meals, and quiet evenings. This order made her feel safe.

Then, the small things started. A book she was reading, left on her bedside table, would turn up on the living room shelf. Her favorite coffee mug, washed and put away, would be found drying on the counter again. At first, Clara just frowned. She told herself she was tired, forgetful. It happened to everyone, right?

But it kept happening. A shoe left by the door would be in her closet. A picture frame on her desk, which she always kept facing her, would be turned around, its glass staring blankly at the wall. Clara started to feel a prickle of unease. She wasn't that forgetful. She knew she wasn't.

One evening, she marked a few items. A small piece of tape on the base of her fruit bowl, another on her toothbrush holder. The next morning, the tape on the fruit bowl was broken, and the bowl itself was a few inches to the left. The toothbrush holder was gone from the bathroom counter, found later under her kitchen sink. Clara's stomach tightened.

She bought a cheap security camera. Set it up in the living room, pointing at the kitchen doorway. She watched the footage every day. Nothing. Hours of still silence, empty rooms. But when she came home, things had still moved. Her keys, which she left on the hall table, would be in her bedroom drawer. Her remote control would be on the bathroom floor. It was like a silent, invisible hand was playing a game with her.

Clara stopped sleeping well. Every creak of the building made her jump. She started keeping a small kitchen knife by her bed. The changes became more personal. Her favorite sweater, folded neatly, would be shoved into a different drawer. Her hairbrush would be resting on her pillow. It felt like someone was touching her things, invading her space, but leaving no trace.

Then she noticed a pattern. Not a fast, sudden shift, but a slow, almost lazy movement. Over days, items were inching their way towards one spot. Not the window, not the door, but under her bed. Her slippers, her spare phone charger, even a small, decorative clock from her shelf – they were all slowly migrating.

One night, a faint scraping sound woke her up. It was light, like something being dragged on carpet. She lay still, heart thumping. The scraping stopped. Clara slowly reached for the light switch. Her room flooded with yellow light. She grabbed her phone, held the kitchen knife tight. She looked at the floor.

Her bed was a bit higher off the ground than usual. It looked as if the legs had been slightly lifted. She saw a gap, bigger than it should be, a dark opening where the bed met the floorboards. She slowly got out of bed, knife held forward. She bent down, shining her phone's light into the darkness.

### Conclusion
Under her bed, there was a hole. Not a small crack, but a perfectly round, smooth opening, about two feet across, cleanly cut into the wooden floor. And inside the hole, not dirt or pipes, but a nest. A soft, dark nest, woven from all her missing things: socks, torn pages from old notebooks, strands of her hair, even the tiny plastic cap from her toothpaste. And nestled right in the center of the strange, soft pile, was a smooth, pale, oval thing. It was about the size of a grapefruit, and it pulsed with a faint, steady beat, like a hidden heart.
