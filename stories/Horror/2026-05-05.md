# The Pinprick

**Genre:** Horror | **Date:** 2026-05-05

### Characters
*   Arthur: A quiet man who lives alone.

### The Story
Arthur was a creature of habit. Every morning, he wore the same kind of plain blue shirt. One Tuesday, as he buttoned up, his finger snagged. He looked down. Right on his chest, a tiny, perfect black dot sat in the fabric. It wasn't a loose thread or a stain. It was a pinprick, a true hole, but the cloth wasn't torn. It looked like it had always been there, just hidden. He shrugged it off. A small flaw.

The next day, Arthur made his usual coffee. He picked up his favorite mug, the one with the chipped rim. As he poured the hot water, he saw it again. Another tiny, perfect black dot. This one was on the white ceramic, near the handle. It was exactly like the one on his shirt. He rubbed it. It was smooth. It wasn't dirt. It was a hole, but not a chip. Just a tiny, deep black point.

He started looking. He found another on the wooden frame of his living room window. One on the cover of a book on his shelf. Even one on the plastic remote control. Each one was small, no bigger than a needle tip. Each one was perfectly round and intensely black, like it swallowed light. They didn't seem to damage anything. They were just... there.

Arthur felt a chill creep into him. These weren't random flaws. They were too alike, too perfect. He tried to ignore them, but they seemed to multiply. Soon, there were dozens in his apartment. A scattering on the wall, a few on the kitchen counter, even a couple on the dusty floorboards. He’d stare at them, feeling a strange pull. It wasn't fear, not yet. It was a deep, quiet wrongness.

One evening, he was sitting on his couch, staring at a small cluster of dots on the wall opposite him. There were four now, arranged in a rough square. As he watched, one of them, the one nearest the ceiling, seemed to... flicker. Not like a light, but like something was moving *inside* it. He leaned forward, eyes wide.

He swore he saw a tiny, dark movement. Like a miniature insect, but too black, too deep. Then, the flicker came again, and this time, he saw a sliver of faint light from *behind* the hole. It wasn't just a hole *in* the wall. It was a hole *through* it, into somewhere else. And something was peering back.

He jumped up, heart pounding. He backed away slowly. The dots were everywhere now. On his clothes, his hands, even on the smooth surface of his phone screen. They weren't just pinpricks anymore. Some were slightly bigger, maybe the size of a peppercorn. And from each, he could feel a faint, cold whisper of nothingness.

He ran to his front door, fumbling with the lock. He needed to get out. He needed to run. But as his hand touched the cold metal doorknob, he saw it. A cluster of holes had bloomed right there, on the metal, arranged like tiny, perfect eyes. And as he stared, one of them widened, just a fraction. Enough for him to see, deep within its blackness, a single, unblinking... eye. Not an eye *looking* out, but an eye *looking in*. An eye that was utterly black, but somehow, seeing him.

### Conclusion
Arthur stumbled back, tripping over the rug. He landed hard. He looked down at his outstretched hand. The small black dots were everywhere on his skin now. Dozens of them, perfectly round, perfectly black. He touched one. It was smooth. And then, he felt a faint pressure from *inside* his finger, pushing outwards, as if something was trying to unbutton him from the inside. He wasn't seeing holes *in* the world. The world was seeing holes *in him*. The pinpricks weren't appearing on surfaces; they were appearing *through* them, from *inside*, and he was the surface.
