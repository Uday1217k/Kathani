# The Chipped Heart

**Genre:** Horror | **Date:** 2026-04-21

### Characters
Alice: A quiet woman who lives by herself.

### The Story
Alice liked her mornings to be just right. The sun, a warm slice on the kitchen floor. The smell of fresh coffee. And her favorite teacup. It was white, with small blue flowers painted all around it. She had used it every day for years.

One Tuesday, Alice saw a tiny chip on the rim of her cup. It was barely there, a small nick in the smooth white edge. "Oh dear," she mumbled. She hated broken things. But it was just a small chip, easy to forget.

The next morning, the chip was bigger. It was not just a nick now, but a piece missing, about the size of her fingernail. Alice frowned. She must have been extra clumsy. She felt a little bad, like she had hurt an old friend.

Days turned into a week. More chips showed up. Not just on the rim, but lower down the cup's side. Some of them appeared overnight. She would leave the cup clean on the drying rack, then find a new chip when she took it down for her tea. Alice started to feel a prickle of worry. Was her eyesight bad? Was she knocking it without knowing?

She put the cup on her table and watched it. Nothing. She looked away for a second, then back. No change. But later that day, when she went to wash it, another piece was gone. This new missing bit was different. The edge was not sharp like broken china. It felt soft, almost rubbery, to her finger. The white part underneath was not white at all. It was a dull, purplish red.

Alice felt a shiver. This was not right. The blue flowers on the cup looked odd, too. They seemed to rise up from the surface now, like thin, winding lines. They looked like tiny, tangled veins.

She held the cup in her hands. It felt warm, much warmer than an empty ceramic cup should be. She watched the smallest, newest 'chip'. It seemed to pulse, a tiny, slow beat, almost too faint to notice. Her breath caught in her throat.

The white ceramic was peeling away now, like old, dry skin. Beneath it, the deep red color spread. The blue flower lines grew thicker, crisscrossing like a map of someone’s insides. The gentle thrumming grew a little stronger in her hands.

### Conclusion
Alice looked at her teacup, no longer a teacup, but a pulsing, soft mass of reddish-purple flesh. The blue patterns were clearly veins. A fresh, soft "chip" peeled away, revealing more of the living, wet organ beneath. Her favorite cup had been slowly, day by day, shedding its ceramic shell, revealing the slow, steady beat of a human heart. And it was still warm in her hands.
