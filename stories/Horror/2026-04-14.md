# The Dustman's Burden

**Genre:** Horror | **Date:** 2026-04-14

### Characters
Arthur (A man who lives alone)

### The Story
Arthur loved a clean home. Not just tidy, but *clean*. Every Saturday morning, without fail, he'd start with his dusting. A soft cloth, a special spray, and a methodical sweep across every surface. The coffee table, the bookshelf, the window sill. Nothing escaped his careful hand.

But the dust was a defiant enemy. No matter how much he cleaned, it was back. By Tuesday, a fine layer would already have settled. By Friday, it was noticeable. It seemed to appear faster than in other homes he'd visited. He’d even bought an air filter, but it did little.

Over the months, Arthur started to feel tired after his dusting routine. Not just physically, but a deep, odd kind of exhaustion. As if something had been taken from him. He’d shrug it off, telling himself it was just the effort of a thorough clean.

One day, as he wiped down his old wooden desk, he noticed something strange in the dust on his cloth. Tiny, glittering flecks. Not like normal dust motes. These caught the light differently, almost like miniature shards of glass. He squinted, trying to make sense of them. They seemed... familiar. But how? He couldn’t place it. He brushed them into the little bag he used for disposal, a small, dark vacuum cleaner bag dedicated solely to dust.

The next week, the flecks were there again. More of them. He examined them under a magnifying glass. They were too small to properly see, just faint glimmering shapes. Yet, a cold dread began to curl in his stomach. Each time he saw them, he felt a flicker of something lost, a faint echo in his mind, like a word on the tip of his tongue that vanished when he tried to grasp it.

He tried to ignore it, to dismiss it as an overactive imagination. But the feeling of being drained grew stronger. His apartment, once a place of comfort, started to feel hollow, even after his meticulous cleaning. He looked at his furniture, his books, his trinkets. They all seemed a little duller, a little less 'there'.

Then came the Saturday when he was dusting his bedside table. A small, simple table where he kept his alarm clock and a framed photo of his parents. As he wiped the dust away, his finger brushed against a tiny speck. It was one of the glittering flecks. This time, it seemed bigger, or maybe he was just looking harder. He picked it up. It was incredibly light, almost weightless. He brought it close to his eye.

And then he saw it.

Inside the tiny, shimmering particle, almost too small to register, was a miniature, distorted image. A flash of memory. His mother’s smile. A specific, long-forgotten moment from his childhood, clear as day, yet gone in an instant, leaving only the dull ache of its absence.

He dropped the fleck. It vanished into the common grey dust on the table. Arthur stared at his hands, then at the dust cloth, then at the half-full vacuum bag he kept for his dust. He staggered backward, knocking over a chair.

The dust wasn’t just dirt. It wasn't just fibers and dead skin. It was his memories. His experiences. Tiny, shimmering fragments of his life, flaking off him, becoming dust. And he, Arthur, the meticulous cleaner, had been diligently collecting them, bagging them up, and carefully throwing them away.

### Conclusion
Arthur looked at his apartment, suddenly understanding why it felt hollow. It wasn’t empty of things, but empty of *him*. Every weekly clean was not making his home spotless; it was slowly emptying his own existence, one glittering memory at a time, into a vacuum bag. He looked down at his hands, seeing a faint shimmer already starting to form on his skin, ready to be swept away next Saturday. The dust wasn’t just a burden he carried; it was him.
