# The Case of the Hairy Zucchini

**Genre:** Comedy | **Date:** 2026-06-14

### Characters
Mildred Piffle: A cheerful, slightly scatterbrained older woman, new to gardening.
Frank Thorn: Mildred's competitive neighbor, a skilled and proud gardener.
Gordon Grumbles: The stern head judge of the annual garden show.
Agnes Crumble: A curious neighbor who always knows everyone's business.

### The Story
Mildred Piffle was not a natural gardener. Her hands were more used to knitting needles than garden tools. But when she moved into her new house, she decided to join the local community garden. Mostly, she wanted to beat Frank Thorn, her next-door neighbor. Frank had won "Best Vegetable" at the annual show for ten years straight with his perfect tomatoes. Frank was a nice man, but he talked a lot about his tomatoes.

Mildred chose a zucchini for her main entry. Zucchinis grew fast, and she figured fast growth meant less time for her to make mistakes. She planted the seeds, watered them well, and waited. But her zucchini plant looked… tired. It wasn't growing as quickly as she had hoped. Frank's tomatoes next door were already big and red.

One afternoon, Mildred saw an ad in a magazine: "Miracle Grow-Max 5000!™ For Unbelievable Growth!" The bottle in the ad showed a thick, green forest. "This is it!" she thought. She ordered a bottle.

A week later, a small package arrived. Mildred excitedly opened the bottle. The liquid inside was thick and smelled a bit like old socks and mint. "Unbelievable Growth, here I come!" she sang. She poured a lot of it around the base of her zucchini plant, making sure the biggest zucchini got a good soak.

The next morning, Mildred rushed to her plot. Her eyes got wide. The zucchini wasn't bigger, not really, but it had… fuzz. Fine, soft fuzz, like a baby's head. "Oh, my! It's a new kind!" she whispered, happy. Frank Thorn, working on his tomatoes, looked over with a confused frown.

Each day, the fuzz grew thicker. Soon, the zucchini had a full, dark green coat. It looked less like a vegetable and more like a very confused, long guinea pig. Then, even stranger, the fuzz on one end began to stick together, making what looked like a tiny, spiky beard. And the other end grew what could only be called a bushy green ponytail.

Agnes Crumble, walking by, stopped completely. "Mildred, dear, what in the world have you done to that poor plant?"

Mildred smiled brightly. "It's my special, secret kind, Agnes! The Miracle Grow-Max 5000!™ really works!"

Frank Thorn made a grunting sound. "It looks like something from a scary movie, Mildred. You'll never win with that monster."

Mildred ignored him. She was sure her hairy zucchini was wonderful.

The day of the annual garden show arrived. Gardeners gathered, showing off their best vegetables. Frank's display of shiny red tomatoes was amazing, as always. Mildred, however, gently placed her hairy zucchini on the judging table. It now had a full, long beard, a clear mustache, and wild, messy green hair, making it look like a grumpy old man who had shrunk and turned green.

Gordon Grumbles, the head judge, a man known for his straight back and even straighter opinions, walked up to Mildred's entry. He fixed his glasses, looked closely, then gasped, stepping back quickly. "Good heavens! What IS that?"

"It's my prize zucchini, Mr. Grumbles!" Mildred said proudly. "A special, hairy kind!"

Frank Thorn pointed a finger. "It's not natural! I demand she be kicked out! It's clearly some kind of bad science experiment, or worse, cheating!"

Mildred felt a rush of anger. "It's no such thing, Frank! It's all thanks to Miracle Grow-Max 5000!™!" She picked up the small, half-empty bottle she'd brought to show and pushed it towards Mr. Grumbles. "See? Unbelievable Growth!"

Mr. Grumbles took the bottle, his forehead wrinkled. He turned it over, reading the tiny words on the back. His eyes got wide, then narrow. He cleared his throat. "Mildred," he began, his voice surprisingly soft. "This product… it clearly says here: 'Miracle Grow-Max 5000!™ – The special hair regrowth formula for hair stimulation. For use on human scalp only. May cause unexpected hair growth on other surfaces.'"

### Conclusion
Mildred stared at the bottle, then at her magnificent, bearded zucchini, and back at the bottle. She hadn't read the tiny words, just the big "Unbelievable Growth!" Her face slowly turned the same shade of green as her zucchini. Frank Thorn groaned and slapped his forehead. Agnes Crumble tried not to laugh. Mr. Grumbles, for the first time anyone could remember, smiled a little. "Well," he said, handing the bottle back, "I suppose it *did* give unbelievable growth, Mildred. Just… not where you thought." And with that, he gave Frank the first-place ribbon, but not before quietly asking Mildred if he could borrow her special "hair tonic" – apparently, his own head was looking a little thin these days.
