# The Neighborly Muse

**Genre:** Comedy | **Date:** 2026-07-12

### Characters
Arthur: A quiet man who enjoys his peace.
Mrs. Higgins: Arthur's friendly, talkative elderly neighbor.

### The Story
Arthur loved his quiet life. He had a small house, a neat garden, and a strong dislike for small talk. His only real challenge came in the form of Mrs. Higgins, his next-door neighbor. Mrs. Higgins was a lovely old woman, but she loved to chat. About her petunias. About her grand-niece’s piano lessons. About the curious habits of squirrels. To Arthur, it was all very… long.

One sunny Tuesday, Arthur was heading out to buy milk when Mrs. Higgins spotted him from her porch. "Arthur, dear! Just the man!" she called, waving a trowel. "Have you seen how wonderfully my petunias are blooming?"

Arthur braced himself. He needed milk, not a plant lecture. "Oh, Mrs. Higgins, I'd love to stop, but I'm in a terrible rush," he said, thinking fast. "My goldfish, Bartholomew, is having his annual swimming lesson. He gets very anxious if I'm late."

Mrs. Higgins paused, trowel in mid-air. "Oh, dear! A swimming lesson for Bartholomew? How very modern! Well, don't let me keep you, then." She smiled, a small, knowing smile, and went back to her flowers. Arthur walked away, feeling rather clever. His goldfish was called Goldie, and she spent her days swimming perfectly fine in her bowl.

A few days later, Mrs. Higgins caught him watering his lawn. "Arthur! I was just wondering if you'd seen the new postman. He seems a bit… lost."

Arthur groaned internally. "I'm so sorry, Mrs. Higgins, but I can't possibly discuss postal routes right now. My garden gnomes are having a very important meeting about sprinkler system efficiency. I’m meant to be the note-taker."

Mrs. Higgins blinked. "Gnomes having a meeting? Oh, my! That does sound urgent. Well, carry on, dear." Her smile was back, a little wider this time. Arthur puffed out his chest a bit. He was quite good at this.

Over the next few months, Arthur invented a series of increasingly wild excuses. He had to rush off because his houseplant, a peace lily, was "experiencing an existential crisis." He once claimed his cat was "auditioning for a role in a local mouse-catching theatre production." Another time, he had to "mediate a fierce debate between his left and right socks over laundry protocol." Each time, Mrs. Higgins would nod, smile, and send him on his way with a cheerful, "Well, don't want to miss that!"

Arthur began to feel like a master of evasive comedy. He often chuckled to himself about Mrs. Higgins's gullibility.

One Saturday, Arthur was at the local bookshop, looking for a new mystery novel. He turned a corner and stopped dead. There, on a table piled high with books, was a large, glossy paperback. The author photo on the back showed a familiar, kindly face: Mrs. Higgins.

The book’s title was "The Curious Case of the Existential Petunias and Other Neighborly Nonsense." Arthur picked it up, his hands shaking slightly. He flipped to the back, reading the blurb. It described a collection of short, humorous stories about a quiet man and his increasingly bizarre excuses to avoid his chatty neighbor. One story was titled "The Goldfish Who Swam to Success." Another: "Gnome Meetings and Laundry Line Debates." Yet another: "The Cat's Big Break: A Theatrical Tale."

### Conclusion
Arthur slowly put the book back on the table. He wasn't a master of evasive comedy. He was Mrs. Higgins's greatest source of inspiration. He hurried out of the shop, suddenly feeling the urgent need to go home and apologize to his goldfish, Goldie. And maybe, just maybe, next time Mrs. Higgins wanted to talk about her petunias, he would actually listen. He certainly owed her one.
