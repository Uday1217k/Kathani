# The Golden Gnome Gaffe

**Genre:** Comedy | **Date:** 2026-06-07

### Characters
Bertram Wiggle: A friendly man, a bit lazy, but surprisingly competitive when it matters.
Martha Evergreen: Bert's neighbor, very neat, takes gardening extremely seriously.
Millicent Finch: The neighborhood judge for the "Golden Gnome" contest, a stern but fair lady.

### The Story
Bertram Wiggle loved his lawn. Not in a fussy way, like some people. He loved it for its soft grass, perfect for napping on a Sunday afternoon. His neighbor, Martha Evergreen, on the other hand, loved her lawn like a prized jewel. Every blade stood at attention, every flower bloomed in perfect order. For three years running, Martha had won the annual "Golden Gnome" award for the best lawn in the entire Blossomwood neighborhood. This year, Bert decided it was his turn.

He started simply. He bought a new bag of "Super Green" lawn food. He watered his grass every evening, even giving it a little pep talk: "Grow strong, lads! Show Martha who's boss!" His lawn, normally a calm, sleepy green, started to look perkier. Martha noticed. She started spending even more time outside. Bert saw her meticulously plucking single, lonely dandelions with tweezers. He saw her polishing the leaves of her prize-winning petunias. This meant war.

Bert upped his game. He hired a college kid, Kevin, to trim his hedges into the shape of a smiling dog. Martha responded by installing tiny LED spotlights that made her prize roses glow like alien flowers at night. Bert bought a special "sonic pest repeller" that buzzed quietly, meant to scare away moles but mostly just annoyed his own cat. Martha, not to be outdone, began wearing a special visor and white gloves while gardening, as if performing surgery on her flower beds. The entire street watched their silent battle with great amusement.

The day of judging arrived. Bert's lawn was a vibrant green, the dog hedge looked goofy but healthy, and his petunias, while not Martha-level, were certainly cheerful. Martha's lawn was a masterpiece of order, every plant symmetrical, every leaf shining. Judge Millicent Finch walked down the street, clipboard in hand, a serious look on her face. She paused at Martha's lawn, nodding slowly, writing notes. Then she moved to Bert's. She walked around the smiling dog hedge, even poked a finger at a particularly vibrant red geranium. Bert held his breath. Martha stood on her porch, arms crossed, a smug smile barely hidden.

Millicent Finch finally stopped in the middle of the street, looked at both lawns, and then, without a word, walked straight to Bert's porch. "Mr. Wiggle," she announced, her voice clear. "This year, the Golden Gnome goes to you." Bert almost fell over. He couldn't believe it! He'd won! Martha gasped loudly from her porch.

### Conclusion
Bert threw his arms up in victory. "Oh, thank you, Judge Finch! I worked so hard!" Millicent Finch gave a small, approving nod. "Yes, well done. And it's perfect, really." She then turned and pointed to the small, rusty, chipped ceramic gnome that Bert had bought at a garage sale years ago and simply plonked near his mailbox. Its hat was askew, its beard was fading. "That gnome," she declared, "is truly the most wonderfully unique lawn ornament I've seen all year. It needed a proper home, and your lawn is just messy enough to make it truly shine."
