# The Grumble in the Floor

**Genre:** Comedy | **Date:** 2026-05-03

### Characters
Bernard: A man who likes order and quiet, easily annoyed.
Carol: His sensible and slightly amused wife.

### The Story
Bernard woke up one morning with a new enemy. It wasn't a spider or a bill. It was a single floorboard in their living room. Right in the middle, between the armchair and the TV, it let out a little *grumble* every time you stepped on it. It wasn't a loud squeak, more of a soft, protesting groan. But to Bernard, it was like a megaphone in his ear.

"Did you hear that?" he asked Carol at breakfast.
Carol, sipping her tea, barely looked up. "Hear what, dear? The birds?"
"No, the *floor*," Bernard insisted. "That board near the sofa. It grumbles."
Carol shrugged. "It's an old house, Bernard. Floors creak."

But Bernard could not let it go. All day, he tried to walk around it. He took a wide detour to get a book, he hopped over it on his way to the kitchen. It was like a tiny, invisible river he had to cross. This worked for about an hour. Then he forgot, stepped on it, and *grumble*. He frowned deeply.

His first plan was simple: put something heavy on it. He found a stack of old magazines and piled them right on the offending board. The next time he stepped near it, the grumble was still there. The magazines just moved with the board, like a tiny boat on a wave.

Next, Bernard decided the board needed lubrication. He went to the kitchen and returned with a bottle of olive oil. Carol watched, wide-eyed, as he carefully poured a thin line of oil into the crack around the board. He then stepped on it. The grumble was gone! He beamed. But in its place was a slippery, oily patch that nearly sent him flying. Carol handed him a paper towel. "Maybe not that," she suggested, very calmly.

Undeterred, Bernard moved to plan C: wedging. He found some thin cardboard from an old cereal box and tried to push it under the edges of the board. The cardboard crumpled. He tried an old plastic ruler. It snapped. He even tried a folded tissue, which just soaked up the leftover olive oil.

Days passed. The grumbling floorboard became Bernard’s personal Everest. He talked to it. "Why do you do this to me?" he’d whisper. He bought a small tube of wood glue and tried to seal the board down. The glue just made a mess, and the grumble was still present, now with sticky dust stuck to the edges. He started wearing soft slippers indoors, hoping the lack of a hard sole would help. It didn't. The grumble seemed to mock him.

One evening, Bernard sat on the sofa, defeated. Carol walked into the living room, heading for the kitchen. She stepped right over the infamous board. No grumble. Bernard stared.
"How did you do that?" he asked, astonished.
Carol stopped, looking confused. "Do what?"
"The board! It didn't grumble!"
She looked down at her feet. "Oh. I took my shoes off when I came in."

### Conclusion
Bernard slowly stood up, looking at his own feet. He was still wearing his outdoor trainers, the ones with the slightly worn rubber sole that had developed a tiny, almost imperceptible *grumble* with every step he took. Carol just blinked at him, then walked into the kitchen, a small smile playing on her lips. Bernard slowly took off his shoes. The living room was perfectly, wonderfully silent.
