# The Unwavering Wake-Up Call

**Genre:** Comedy | **Date:** 2026-07-05

### Characters
*   Gary: A man in his mid-thirties, always running late.
*   The "Rise & Shine 5000": A very advanced alarm clock.

### The Story
Gary was a nice man. He had a good heart and a kind smile. But he had one big problem: mornings. Waking up was like fighting a sticky monster made of blankets and sleep. His bedside table was a graveyard of broken alarm clocks. Some had been thrown across the room. Others had their batteries pulled out in a foggy, half-awake rage.

"Never again!" he vowed, rubbing his tired eyes after missing an important meeting. He saw an ad online for the "Rise & Shine 5000." It promised to end sleepy mornings forever. "Finally," Gary thought, "a smart alarm for a smart guy like me."

The Rise & Shine 5000 looked like a sleek black box. No snooze button. Just a big screen that glowed. Gary set it for 7 AM.

The next morning, at exactly 7:00, the clock screamed. Not a gentle beep, but a piercing, siren-like yell. Gary jolted upright. "Alright, alright, I'm up!" he grumbled, fumbling for the off button. The screen said, "Solve the math problem." Gary, still mostly asleep, stared at "47 + 13 - 5 * 2 = ?" He slowly typed the answer. The alarm stopped. Gary felt a strange mix of annoyance and pride. He was only five minutes late for work. A new record!

Day two. 7 AM. The alarm blasted a heavy metal song. Gary thrashed around. He found the clock trying to hide under his pillow. This time, it asked him to find three matching symbols on the screen. He tapped quickly. The music cut off. He made it to work on time. He even had time for a quick coffee. This was… working?

Day three. Gary braced himself. At 7 AM, a robotic voice boomed, "Good morning, Gary! Time to face the day!" Then, a small, bouncy ball shot out of the clock. It rolled around his room, gently bumping his feet and then bouncing off the wall near his head. He had to catch the ball and place it back into a slot on the clock to stop the voice. It was silly, but it woke him up fully. He was early for work!

This went on for a week. The Rise & Shine 5000 got more and more creative. It once played a clip of his boss saying, "Gary, are you *sure* you know what you're doing?" It another time filled his room with the smell of freshly brewed coffee, which made him desperate to get out of bed. He was never late. In fact, he was now the first one in the office. He looked fresh, awake, and slightly confused.

One Saturday, Gary decided to sleep in. He forgot to turn off the alarm. At 7 AM, the clock went wild. It played loud bird sounds, then a foghorn, then a choir. A tiny mechanical arm popped out, gently tickling his nose. Gary just wanted it to stop. He waved his hand at it. "Off! Off, you crazy box!"

The clock stopped. Then, the screen changed. It showed a message.

"Good morning, Gary! Your account has been charged $5 for exceeding your permitted sleep time."

Gary blinked. "Charged?"

### Conclusion
He grabbed the small instruction booklet. On page four, in very tiny letters, it said: "The Rise & Shine 5000 uses a smart incentive system. For every instance of not responding to the first three alarms, or attempting to disable the clock without solving the daily task, a small, non-refundable 'motivation fee' will be applied to your linked bank account. Consistency is key!" Gary stared at the clock, then at his bank statement. He was perfectly on time now, yes. But his bank balance looked like it had also been woken up – very rudely. "You sneaky little box," he whispered, a smile slowly creeping onto his face. He'd never been so annoyed, or so punctual.
