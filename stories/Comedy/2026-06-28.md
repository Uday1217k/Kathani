# Smoothie or Soup?

**Genre:** Comedy | **Date:** 2026-06-28

### Characters
Barry: A middle-aged man, keen to try new gadgets, a little impatient.
Carol: Barry's wife, calm and observant.

### The Story
Barry was a man who loved a good deal, especially on kitchen stuff. So, when he saw the "ZestMaster 3000" blender advertised as making the "most zesty and surprising drinks ever," he bought it on the spot. He imagined cool, fruity drinks for himself and his wife, Carol. She loved a good smoothie.

That Saturday morning, Barry pulled the ZestMaster from its box. It was sleek and shiny, with lots of buttons. He quickly scanned the picture-heavy instruction manual, mostly looking at the part that showed colorful fruit. "Right," he mumbled to himself, "smoothie time!"

He tossed in a ripe banana, a handful of bright red strawberries, a dollop of yogurt, and some fresh milk. He pressed the button clearly marked "Smoothie." The machine hummed to life with a satisfying whir. Lights flashed, a small indicator glowed green, then red, then purple. Barry watched, pleased. This was going to be excellent.

The blending stopped. Barry poured the thick, reddish liquid into a glass. He took a sniff. His brow furrowed. It smelled… odd. Not like strawberries and banana. More like… a spicy Italian restaurant. He took a hesitant sip. His eyes widened, then he spluttered, barely stopping himself from spitting it out. It tasted exactly like warm tomato soup, but with chunks of blended banana. "What in the blazes?" he gasped. "Zesty? This is... bizarre!"

Determined, Barry cleaned the ZestMaster. He blamed the milk or maybe too much yogurt. This time, he only put in fruit – just bananas and oranges. He pressed "Smoothie" again. The whirring, flashing, and light show repeated. He poured the new concoction. It was orange this time, but the same savory, herby smell filled the kitchen. He tasted it. Still tomato soup, but now with a strong hint of citrus. It was truly awful.

Carol walked into the kitchen, drawn by the strange smells. "Morning, dear," she said, eyeing the two glasses of strange liquid and Barry's confused face. "What are you making? It smells like a pizza parlor exploded."

Barry threw his hands up. "This ZestMaster thing is broken! It's supposed to make fruit smoothies, but it keeps making tomato soup! It's clearly defective!"

Carol walked over to the shiny new blender. She picked up the box, then peered at the machine. "Ah," she said, her finger pointing to a small, clear chamber on the side of the blender, almost hidden by the main body. Inside, a little, cube-shaped packet sat. The packet was labeled: "Spicy Tomato & Basil Flavor Pod." Carol smiled. "It looks like your 'zesty' isn't coming from the fruit, Barry. This ZestMaster automatically adds a flavor pod to every blend. It came with a sample one already loaded."

### Conclusion
Barry looked at the tiny compartment, then back at the "Spicy Tomato & Basil" pod. He had completely missed it in his rush. The "zesty and surprising drinks" promised on the box referred to the wide range of savory flavor pods, not just fruit. His face went from confusion to a slow, sheepish grin. "So," he said, picking up the tomato-banana smoothie. "Anyone for a very experimental gazpacho?"
