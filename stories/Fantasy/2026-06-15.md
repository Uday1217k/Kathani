# The Echoing Stone

**Genre:** Fantasy | **Date:** 2026-06-15

### Characters
*   Elara: A young woman, quiet and practical.
*   Old Man Tiber: The village elder, guardian of the Stone.
*   Marta: Elara's elderly neighbor.

### The Story
Elara lived in a small village among green hills. Her days were simple, filled with helping her family with chores and picking berries in the woods. She often felt like a plain grey pebble next to shiny river stones.

In the middle of the village stood the Echoing Stone. It was a tall, smooth black rock, made shiny by rain and time. People said it showed you a small, true piece of yourself. Not what would happen to you, not what had happened, but what you truly carried inside. Farmers saw rich soil, smiths saw hot coals, weavers saw tangled threads. People would whisper about what they saw, trying to guess what it meant for them.

Elara had always been afraid to look. What if she saw nothing? What if she saw something dull? But today, she felt brave. Old Man Tiber, the village elder, sat by the Stone, his eyes kind. "Ready, Elara?" he asked, his voice like dry leaves.

She nodded, her heart beating fast like a drum. She walked to the Stone, touching its cool, smooth face. She closed her eyes for a moment, wishing to see something big and important. A bird flying high, a tall mountain, a bright star. Anything to make her feel less like a plain pebble.

When she opened her eyes and looked into the dark surface, she saw it. Not a reflection of her face, but a picture floating inside the stone.

It was a small, plain wooden spoon.

Elara frowned. A wooden spoon? She cooked sometimes, she ate with spoons, but it was not special. It was not *her*. It felt so… ordinary. Her shoulders dropped. She stepped back from the Stone.

Old Man Tiber gave her a soft smile. "What did you see, child?"

"A wooden spoon," Elara mumbled, feeling silly. "Just a spoon."

Tiber chuckled, a quiet sound in his chest. "Ah, the Stone shows strange things. Tell me, what do you do each evening after your chores?"

Elara thought. "I take a bowl of hot soup to old Marta. Her hands shake too much to cook for herself."

"And what does old Marta use to eat that soup?" Tiber asked, his eyes shining.

Elara paused. Marta had a favorite old wooden spoon. It was the only one she could hold steady. Elara always placed it in her hand.

### Conclusion
A sudden warmth spread through Elara. The Stone had not shown her what *she* carried. It had shown her what *Marta* carried because of Elara: a simple wooden spoon, filled daily with comfort and care. Elara realized she wasn't a plain pebble at all. She was the quiet stream that helped the river flow. And that, she found, was better than any soaring bird or tall mountain. She carried kindness, and it made all the difference to someone else.
