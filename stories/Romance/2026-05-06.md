# The Blue Pen Pact

**Genre:** Romance | **Date:** 2026-05-06

### Characters
Clara: A freelance writer, a bit messy, loves her specific blue pen.
Leo: A regular coffee shop customer, observant and quiet.

### The Story
Clara always used the same blue pen. It had a nice feel, smooth ink. She took it everywhere, especially to The Daily Grind, her favorite coffee shop. She’d sit at her usual table, writing stories on her laptop. But Clara was also a little bit forgetful. A lot, actually.

One Tuesday, she packed up her things. Laptop, charger, notebook. She walked out, already thinking about her next paragraph. The next morning, she needed her blue pen. It wasn’t in her bag. Clara groaned. She knew where it was. The Daily Grind.

She went back. “Excuse me,” she asked the barista, “did anyone turn in a blue pen?”
“Oh, the one with the silver clip?” a voice said from behind her.
Clara turned. A man stood there, holding her pen. He had kind eyes and a small smile. “I saw it on your table after you left yesterday. I figured it was important.”
“It is!” Clara felt a warm blush. “Thank you so much. My name’s Clara.”
“Leo,” he replied, handing it over. Their fingers brushed. It felt nice.

A week later, it happened again. Clara was deep in thought, then rushed off. This time, she didn’t even notice. But as she walked out, Leo was there, holding her pen. “Again?” he chuckled.
Clara laughed, feeling silly. “I’m hopeless, aren’t I?”
“Just focused,” Leo said, his smile soft. They chatted for a few minutes about what she was writing. Clara found herself liking these little pen rescues.

It became a thing. Every now and then, Clara would forget her pen. And every time, Leo would appear, pen in hand, a knowing look on his face. Sometimes he’d be sitting at a nearby table. Other times, he seemed to just materialize as she was leaving. Their quick chats grew longer. They started to talk about books, movies, their lives. Clara looked forward to seeing him. She found herself subconsciously checking her table less often. It was nice knowing Leo would be there. These 'pen moments' were how they got to know each other.

One sunny afternoon, they were having coffee together – a proper meet-up, not a pen rescue. Clara picked up her blue pen, twirling it. “You know,” she said, “I think this pen is enchanted. It keeps making me run into you.”
Leo chuckled, taking a sip of his coffee. “It does, doesn’t it?” He paused, a different kind of smile on his face. “There’s something I should tell you about that pen.”
Clara raised an eyebrow. “Oh?”

### Conclusion
Leo leaned forward a little. “After the first time I gave it back to you, I noticed how much you liked it, and how easily you forgot it. And I really liked talking to you.” He took a breath. “So, sometimes, when you got really deep into writing, I would…borrow it. Just for a moment. Then I’d put it somewhere obvious, so I could return it. Or, sometimes, when I saw you packing up, I’d ever so gently nudge it off your notebook before you noticed.” He winked. “It was the only way I could guarantee another chance to talk to you.”
Clara stared, then burst out laughing. It wasn't annoying or strange. It was sweet, and a little bit sneaky. She felt a warm, bubbly feeling spread through her. “You mean,” she said, still laughing, “all those times… you were behind it?”
Leo nodded, a genuine, hopeful look on his face. “Only because I really wanted to keep finding you.”
Clara smiled, a big, bright smile. “Well,” she said, holding up her blue pen, “it worked.”
