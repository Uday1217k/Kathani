# The Wrong Door

**Genre:** Thriller | **Date:** 2026-07-24

### Characters
*   Mark: A young man, lives alone in an apartment.
*   The Man: A locksmith, prone to mixing up numbers.
*   Mrs. Evans: Mark's next-door neighbor.

### The Story
Mark lived on the fourth floor. Apartment 4A. It was a quiet building, usually. Or so he thought.

First, it was little things. A pizza box left by his door, clearly marked for 4B. Mark shrugged. Delivery guys make mistakes. Then, he saw a man. The man stood outside Mark’s door. He stared at the number plate, then at Mark’s door, then down the hall. He looked confused, maybe a little angry. Mark went inside, feeling a bit odd about it.

A few days later, Mark was watching TV. He heard a soft scraping sound. It came from his front door. He froze. The sound stopped. Mark waited, holding his breath. Nothing else happened. He told himself it was just the building settling. Or a mouse.

Then, he came home to find his doorknob jiggled. He was sure he’d locked it tight. He always did. He checked the locks again. He started to feel a chill. He bought a small camera for his peephole.

The camera showed him nothing for two days. Then, late one night, it recorded it. The same man. He was at Mark’s door. He held a small metal tool. He was trying to push it into the keyhole. Mark watched the video. His blood ran cold. This wasn't a mouse. This was someone trying to get in.

Mark couldn't sleep. He called the police. They said they would send someone to patrol. But the man didn't come back that night. Mark felt watched even when he was inside his apartment. Every shadow looked bigger. Every sound made him jump.

He decided he wouldn't just wait. He had to face this. He kept a heavy book near his door. Just in case. He saw the man again two nights later. He was outside Mark’s door. His back was to Mark. He was bent over the lock. He was muttering under his breath. He had the tool again.

Mark took a deep breath. He grabbed the book. He opened his door fast. “Hey!” he yelled.

The man jumped. He spun around. He looked startled, almost scared. He dropped his metal tool on the floor.

Mark stood firm. "What do you want?" he asked, his voice shaking a little.

### Conclusion
The man stared. He looked like he'd seen a ghost. "Oh, you're the one in 4A," he said, sounding tired. He ran a hand over his face. "Finally. Listen, I'm just trying to get into 4B. Mrs. Evans hired me. Dyslexia, see? Can't tell my 'A's from my 'B's, especially with these tricky building numbers. I keep thinking this is the one. And this lock is stubborn." He picked up his tool. "I'm a locksmith, mate. Not a burglar. She thinks someone tried to break in last week. Guess that was me too. Wrong door. Again." He looked at the floor, shaking his head.

Mark stood still, the heavy book now feeling useless in his hand. The chill he felt slowly turned into a wave of pure, red-faced embarrassment. The "threat" was a tired man who couldn't read numbers well, not a dangerous criminal. All the fear, the sleepless nights, the calls to the police – it was all for a locksmith who just kept knocking on the wrong door. He watched the man sigh and walk two doors down, muttering about "tricky numbers" as he finally found 4B. Mark just closed his door, the silence in his apartment suddenly feeling very, very loud.
