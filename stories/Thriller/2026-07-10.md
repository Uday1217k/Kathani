# The Red Pen's Secret

**Genre:** Thriller | **Date:** 2026-07-10

### Characters
*   Sarah: A quiet worker in a book finishing plant.
*   Mr. Henderson: The strict shift supervisor.
*   Brenda: Sarah's friendly co-worker.
*   Mr. Jenkins: A maintenance worker (minor role).
*   The Red Pen: An unknown person leaving notes.

### The Story
Sarah worked in the big book factory. Her job was to check books. She made sure the covers were straight and the pages were all there. It was a simple, quiet job. Most days, nothing exciting happened.

One Tuesday morning, she picked up a thick novel. Ready to check it, she saw a tiny piece of paper tucked inside the first few pages. It was folded small. She opened it. Neatly written in red ink were three words: "The red pen is watching." Sarah felt a small shiver. She used a red pen to mark errors. Was it a joke? A new worker playing a prank? She crumpled the note and threw it away.

The next day, another book, another note. This time, it said: "He moves the clock back." Sarah thought about Mr. Jenkins, the maintenance man. He often fiddled with the big factory clock. Sometimes it was a few minutes off. She frowned. This felt less like a prank.

Over the next week, the notes kept coming. Always in different books, always in the same neat red handwriting. "Your coffee mug, empty by 10 AM." (It always was.) "The light flicker, it's not the bulb." (She had noticed a light in her work area blinking on and off). Sarah started to feel eyes on her. She looked around. Everyone was busy, moving books, stacking boxes. No one seemed to notice her.

She tried to talk to Brenda, who worked next to her. "Someone's leaving me notes," Sarah whispered. Brenda just laughed. "Probably Dave from shipping. He's always pulling pranks. Ignore him." But Sarah couldn't ignore them. The notes were too specific. They knew too much about her day.

Then came a note that made her blood run cold: "He'll be at your station, next Tuesday, for the final check. Don't touch the *yellow* cover." Sarah reread the words. A "final check" meant Mr. Henderson, the stern shift supervisor. He rarely came to her station. And yellow-covered books? They were very rare.

Tuesday arrived, heavy and slow. Sarah's hands felt clumsy. She kept looking at the clock. At ten past ten, Mr. Henderson appeared. He walked straight to her work area. On the conveyor belt, a small stack of books with bright yellow covers moved towards her. Her heart hammered. Mr. Henderson picked up the first yellow book. He looked at it, then at Sarah. He held her gaze for a moment, a tiny smile on his face, before turning back to the books. Sarah felt a cold dread spread through her. She didn't touch the yellow books. She just watched him.

### Conclusion
That Monday, the day before the yellow books were due, Sarah found one last note. It was folded so small, tucked deep inside the spine of a thick history book, she almost missed it. This one was longer, more rushed. It said: "He knows I'm telling you. He checks the books before they reach you. This is the last one. The yellow books, Tuesday. He puts something in them. Find out what. Look inside the binding, the *very first* one he touches."

Tuesday. Mr. Henderson stood at her station. He picked up the first yellow-covered book. He looked it over, then, with a quick, smooth movement, he slid a tiny, flat object into the spine of the book before signing off on it. He gave Sarah another one of his strange, knowing smiles, and walked away.

Sarah's hands shook. As soon as he was out of sight, she grabbed the yellow book. She tore at the binding, just as the note had said. Hidden deep within the spine was a small, tightly folded piece of paper. She pulled it out. It was a list. Names and dates. And next to each name, a series of numbers. Phone numbers.

She scanned the list, her eyes racing. Her own name was there, with today's date, and a phone number she didn't know. She flipped the paper over. In the same neat, red handwriting, it simply said: "These are the people he's getting rid of. They question the 'quality control.' I made sure *you* would find this. - The Red Pen."
