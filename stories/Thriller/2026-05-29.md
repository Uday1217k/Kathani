# The Guest Room

**Genre:** Thriller | **Date:** 2026-05-29

### Characters
*   Clara: A young, independent woman, new to the city and the apartment.
*   Leo: Clara's helpful, friendly neighbor across the hall.
*   Mr. Henderson: The building manager, a gruff and straightforward man.

### The Story
Clara loved her new apartment. It was small, but the afternoon sun hit the living room just right, making everything feel warm and bright. She spent her first week unpacking boxes, humming along to music. Life felt good, full of new beginnings.

Then the little things started. A kitchen drawer she knew she had closed was open a crack. Her favorite coffee mug was in the sink when she was sure she had washed it the night before and put it away. She blamed it on moving stress, being tired, or just plain forgetfulness.

A few days later, it got stranger. A half-eaten sandwich appeared on her kitchen counter. She lived alone. She definitely hadn't bought that kind of bread. She stared at it, her stomach twisting. Had she bought it and just forgotten? No, impossible. This was rye. She hated rye bread.

She told Leo, her friendly neighbor from across the hall, about it. He chuckled. "Old buildings have quirks, Clara. Drafts, maybe a mouse. Don't worry too much." But Leo's easy smile didn't ease her mind at all.

Clara bought a cheap camera from a discount store. She set it up in the living room, pointing towards her kitchen. It was only meant to record when it sensed movement. She left for work the next day feeling a tight knot in her stomach.

That evening, she got home and rushed to check the camera. Her hands shook as she scrolled through the footage. Most of it was just her, moving about her day. Then, there it was. In the middle of the night, a faint, blurry shape moved from the small, unused second bedroom – a 'guest room' she hadn't bothered to unpack – into the kitchen. It was quick, quiet. Too blurry to see a face, but it was definitely a person. A person in her apartment.

Clara screamed. Not a loud, dramatic scream, but a choked, silent one that caught in her throat. She scrambled for her phone and called the police. They came, looked around, and found no forced entry, no signs of a break-in. They looked at the footage. "Could be a glitch, ma'am. Or a reflection." They left her feeling silly, but still deeply terrified.

She couldn't sleep. She spent the night sitting on her sofa, a heavy book clutched in her hand, staring at the guest room door. Nothing moved. Nothing stirred.

The next morning, driven by a raw, cold fear, she went to the guest room. The door was slightly ajar, just like in the video. She pushed it open slowly. The room was mostly empty, still filled with some boxes. But on the single old bed, pushed against the far wall, was a rumpled blanket. And next to it, on the dusty bedside table, was a small, neatly folded piece of paper.

Her heart pounded. She picked it up. It was a note, written in neat, small handwriting.

*Sorry about the sandwich. I know you don't like rye. I'll remember next time. – Your other tenant.*

Clara dropped the note. She ran out of the apartment, down the stairs, and straight to Mr. Henderson, the building manager's office. He was a gruff man, rarely smiled.

"Mr. Henderson, someone is in my apartment! Living there! I found a note!" Clara babbled, thrusting the paper at him.

Mr. Henderson read the note. His expression didn't change. He simply looked up at her, his eyes unblinking.

### Conclusion
"Ah," he said, handing the note back. "That explains it. The last tenant moved out almost a year ago, but they never *really* left. You see, this building has a peculiar layout. Your apartment, unit 4B, is actually split. Half of it is 4B, and the other half… well, the other half is 4A. A smaller, cheaper unit that also shares *your* kitchen and that 'guest room' you mentioned. We usually try to rent 4A out first, but it's hard to fill with the shared amenities. We assumed the last tenant in 4A had moved on. Looks like they just moved into your guest room, thinking it was still their space, waiting for a new 4A tenant to arrive." He paused, a hint of a grim smile playing on his lips. "It's all on your lease, of course. Check page seven. Did you… miss that part?"
