# The Quiet House

**Genre:** Thriller | **Date:** 2026-04-24

### Characters
*   Sarah: A woman who feels she is being watched and hunted.
*   Emily: Sarah's sister, caring and concerned.

### The Story
Sarah woke up. The old house was too quiet. A week ago, she came back here. After a long time away, every sound made her jump. The floorboards creaked. The wind outside whined low. She felt eyes on her, always.

Yesterday, she found the back door. It was open a crack. She knew she had locked it tight. Her heart beat fast. She pushed it shut and locked it again, checking twice.

Later, the phone rang. She picked it up. Nothing. Just a soft hiss. Then, the line went dead. She stared at the phone. Who was playing games? Or was someone just checking if she was home?

At night, she looked out her window. A dark shape stood near the old oak tree. Tall. Still. Watching. She pulled the curtains quickly. Her breath caught in her chest. They were here. The people from her past. The bad deal. They were here. She had to run.

She grabbed her car keys from the hook. Her fingers trembled. Wait. The hook was empty. The keys were gone. Panic rose like a wave. She dropped to her knees, looking under the table. Nothing. She searched her coat, her bag. No keys.

She ran to her bedroom. Locked the door. Pulled a heavy chair in front of it. Her phone showed no signal. She felt trapped. The house was no longer safe. It was a cage. She heard a noise downstairs. A soft thud. Then, footsteps. Slow. Coming up the stairs.

She grabbed the heavy lamp beside her bed. It was solid, good for hitting. The footsteps stopped outside her door. The doorknob turned. Slowly. She held her breath. The door pushed open a little, hitting the chair. A hand pushed harder. The chair scraped back. The door opened more.

### Conclusion
A woman stepped in. Her face was soft, worried. It was Emily, her sister. Emily looked around the messy room, at the lamp in Sarah's shaking hand. "Sarah," Emily said, her voice gentle. "What are you doing? You locked me out again. And your medicine... it's gone from the kitchen counter. Did you take it?"
