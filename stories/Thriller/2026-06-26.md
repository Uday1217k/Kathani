# The Red Umbrella Chase

**Genre:** Thriller | **Date:** 2026-06-26

### Characters
Leo: A quiet man who works with numbers. He likes his life to be simple.
Tom: Leo's long-time friend, always full of ideas.

### The Story
Leo liked his quiet life. Every morning, he woke up, made coffee, and walked to his job at the accounting firm. Every evening, he walked home. He ate dinner, read a book, and went to bed. This was his normal, and he liked it very much.

One rainy Tuesday, Leo was on the bus. He looked out the window. Across the street, a woman waited for the light to change. She held a bright red umbrella. It was a strong, eye-catching red. Leo didn't think much of it. Many people had red umbrellas.

The next day, the sun was out. Leo was grabbing his lunch from a small cafe near his office. As he walked back, he saw it again. Leaning against a lamp post outside the cafe was the same bright red umbrella. It was open, drying in the sun, but no one was near it. He thought it was strange. Who leaves an umbrella like that?

Two days later, Leo felt a shiver. He was walking home late, after working extra hours. The streetlights made long shadows. He looked up at the sky, checking for rain. That’s when he saw it. A man, walking ahead of him on the same street, held the bright red umbrella. The man looked back for a moment. Leo quickly turned his head away. Was it the same umbrella? Or was he just seeing things?

Over the next few days, the red umbrella became a part of Leo’s life. He saw it on the subway. He saw it in a shop window. He even thought he saw it in the background of a news report on TV. It was always that bright, unmistakable red. He started to feel watched. He started to look over his shoulder. Was someone following him? Was this some kind of message? But what message? And from whom?

Leo changed his routine. He took different bus routes. He left work early, then late. But no matter what he did, he felt like the red umbrella was always close by. He lost sleep. His simple life was gone. Now, every red thing made him jump. Every person carrying an umbrella made him look twice. He knew it was silly, but he couldn't stop. He felt a knot in his stomach all the time.

One evening, Leo was almost home. He put his key in the lock of his apartment building door. He looked up. There it was. Leaning against the wall, right next to his door, was the bright red umbrella. It was closed, but the color shouted at him. His heart beat fast. This was too much. This was real. Someone knew where he lived. Someone wanted him to know they were here.

Leo gripped the umbrella. It felt heavier than it looked. He felt a small bump inside the handle. He pulled on it. A small, rolled-up piece of paper came out. Just as he saw it, a voice boomed from behind him.

### Conclusion
"You finally picked it up, Leo! It took you long enough!"

Leo spun around, nearly dropping the umbrella. His friend, Tom, stood there, grinning, holding his phone. "Level one complete! Man, I owe Mark twenty bucks. He said you'd crack today, but I thought you'd last longer."

Leo just stared, his mouth open. Tom laughed. "Remember our old college game? The 'Object Hunt'? This year, the clue was a bright red umbrella. We've been leaving it around for three days, trying to get you to notice! You kept walking past it!" Tom pointed to the small paper in Leo's hand. "Now, that little note tells you where to find the next one."
