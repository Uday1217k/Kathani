# The Other Clara

**Genre:** Thriller | **Date:** 2026-06-12

### Characters
*   Clara: A young woman, the main character.
*   Tom: Clara's boyfriend, lives with her.

### The Story
Clara first saw her across the street. A woman with her hair, her scarf, even her quick way of walking. Clara blinked. The woman turned into a shop doorway and was gone. Clara shook her head. Too much coffee, maybe. Or not enough sleep.

A week later, it happened again. Clara was waiting for her morning coffee. She looked out the window and saw the woman. Same hair, same scarf. She was looking at the bakery next door, her hand resting on her chin, just like Clara did when she was thinking. Clara watched her walk away, a cold feeling spreading in her stomach. It was too close to be a coincidence.

Days turned into a blur of unease. Clara saw her everywhere. In the park, sitting on *her* usual bench. Inside the bookstore, flipping through the new arrivals. Once, she saw the woman standing at the bus stop, holding a blue umbrella. Clara didn't own a blue umbrella. But she *did* have one, exactly like it, that Tom had given her. It was in the closet. Wasn't it?

She told Tom. He just held her hand, looking kind. "You're stressed, sweetie," he said. "Work's been rough. Maybe you need a break." He didn't believe her. Clara felt a sharp stab of anger, mixed with growing fear. Was she going crazy? Or was someone playing a cruel game?

The sightings got worse. More personal. She saw the woman in her own building's lobby, picking up mail. She saw her leaving *their* apartment door. Clara froze. She knew that coat. It was *her* coat. But she was inside the apartment, trying to make sense of things. She ran out, heart hammering. The hallway was empty.

Clara started checking the locks multiple times. She kept the blinds closed. Sleep was impossible. Every shadow seemed to hold the gaze of that other woman. She began to suspect Tom. He was acting odd, watching her when he thought she wasn't looking. He was too calm, too dismissive. Maybe he was in on it. Maybe he was trying to make her doubt her own mind.

One evening, searching for her lost phone charger, Clara found something small and black stuck to the underside of their living room bookshelf. A tiny lens, a blinking red light. A camera. Hidden.

Her breath caught in her throat. She ripped it off the shelf. Tom. It had to be Tom. He was watching her. He was involved. The other woman... she was just a distraction, a part of their sick game.

She gripped the camera, her knuckles white. She waited for Tom to come home, heart pounding like a drum. The front door opened. Tom walked in, smiling tiredly. "Rough day, hon," he said, dropping his bag.

"Who is she?" Clara whispered, holding up the camera. "Who is the other woman? And why are you watching me?"

Tom's smile faded. He looked at the camera, then at Clara's wild eyes. He walked to her slowly, calmly. "Clara, please, let's sit down."

"No!" she yelled. "Tell me! What are you doing?"

Tom sighed, a deep, sad sound. He took the camera gently from her hand. "I wasn't watching you to hurt you, Clara. I was watching you because I was worried." He pulled out his phone. "Look," he said, pressing play on a video.

The screen showed Clara. *Her* Clara. The woman in her own living room, holding a blue umbrella. She walked around, seemed to be looking for something. Then she opened the front door, peered into the hallway, then closed it. All while Clara, *this* Clara, was sure she'd been on the sofa watching TV.

"I found this yesterday," Tom said, his voice soft. "It was in the closet, with your keys and wallet." He held up the blue umbrella. "You left the house for twenty minutes, then came back. You don't remember any of it, do you?"

Clara stared at the video, then at the umbrella, then at Tom's kind, sad eyes. The woman on the screen... it *was* her. It had always been her. Her mind, so sure it was being hunted, had built a ghost out of her own forgotten moments. The memory gaps. The lost items. The strange feelings.

### Conclusion
Tom gently put a small, orange pill bottle on the table. "Your new sleeping pills, Clara. I read the side effects online. 'Short-term memory disruption. And sometimes, doing things you won't remember.' You've been getting up, going places, and just forgetting it all." Clara looked at the bottle, then at the camera, then at her own trembling hands. The hunter wasn't out there. The ghost wasn't real. The terror had come from inside her own head, a silent enemy born of a simple pill. And for the first time in weeks, she felt both utterly alone and terrifyingly exposed.
