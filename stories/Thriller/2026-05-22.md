# The Lost Page

**Genre:** Thriller | **Date:** 2026-05-22

### Characters
*   Sarah: A quiet, observant librarian.
*   John: A nervous man.
*   Emily: Sarah's younger sister, presumed dead for years.

### The Story
Sarah loved her job at the quiet town library. Days passed in a calm hum of turning pages and hushed whispers. She liked putting things in their right place, both books and the small, unnoticed details of life. She often saw more than people thought, like the faint coffee stain on Mrs. Henderson's usual romance novel, or the nervous twitch in Mr. Gable's eye when he checked out books on old coins.

One Tuesday, a man Sarah had never seen before came in. He was young, dressed in a plain grey jacket, and looked around with wide, scared eyes. He picked up a thick history book, went to a table in the back, and sat down. He didn't read. He just kept looking at the door, then his watch. After ten minutes, he stood up fast, left the book open on the table, and hurried out. Sarah watched him go, a strange feeling in her gut. He had left the book right open. She waited a moment, then went to pick it up.

As she closed the heavy cover, a small piece of paper fluttered out. It was a folded square, no bigger than her thumb. On it, written in neat but shaky handwriting, was a single word: "FIND." Underneath, a tiny, almost invisible speck of red, like a drop of dried blood, was stuck to the paper. Sarah turned the paper over, then opened the book again. She ran her fingers along the spine, a habit from years of checking for loose pages or hidden notes. Nothing. She put the paper in her pocket.

Over the next few days, Sarah saw the nervous man again. Not in the library, but outside. Once, he walked past the bakery where she got her lunch. Another time, he was waiting at the bus stop, staring blankly ahead. Each time, he looked more tired, more afraid. And each time, Sarah felt a shiver down her back. Was he looking for her? Did he know she had the paper? She didn't know what to do. The word "FIND" kept spinning in her head.

One evening, after the library closed, Sarah took the history book home. She studied it carefully under a bright lamp. She turned every page, checked every illustration. Nothing. Then she remembered the red speck on the paper. It wasn't blood. It looked like a tiny fragment of something. She took a small magnifying glass and looked at the book's spine again, both inside and out. Her fingers brushed against a tiny bump, almost at the bottom, near the last few pages. It was barely there. With a careful librarian's tool, she gently pried open the binding. Inside, hidden deep in the glue and paper, was a small, rolled-up piece of very thin film. Not a note, not a map, but something almost like a tiny photograph.

The next morning, Sarah knew she had to show someone. She decided to find the nervous man first. Maybe he was in trouble. She went to the bus stop where she'd seen him last. He was there, looking even worse. His face was pale, his shoulders hunched. Sarah walked up to him. "Excuse me," she said softly. "Are you alright?" The man jumped, fear clear in his eyes. He looked around wildly. "You... you have it, don't you?" he whispered, his voice thin. "The book." Sarah nodded, pulling the history book from her bag. Before she could say anything else, he grabbed her arm. "They know you found it," he gasped. "They're watching. Run!" Just then, a black car with tinted windows pulled up to the curb. The man screamed and tried to pull away, but two figures in dark clothes were already out of the car. Sarah dropped the book and ran as fast as she could.

She ran all the way home, heart pounding. She was trapped. They knew about her. They were after the film. She locked her door, pulled down the blinds. She felt like a fish caught in a net. The film was small, but whatever it showed must be very important. She sat on her sofa, the tiny film clutched in her hand, breathing hard. Then her phone rang. It was an unknown number. Sarah hesitated, then answered. A low, calm voice spoke. "Hello, Sarah. Did you find it?" Sarah froze. "Who is this?" she whispered. The voice chuckled. "Oh, Sarah. Always so careful. You have the film, don't you? The one from the book. The real page, as I like to call it." Sarah looked at the film. It was so tiny. "How do you know about that?" The voice grew harder. "Because I put it there. And I knew you, my dear sister, would be the one to find it. You always were so good at finding what was lost, weren't you?"

### Conclusion
Sarah's hand trembled, dropping the phone. The voice on the other end was clear now, like a shard of ice in her ear. It was Emily. Her younger sister, who had vanished without a trace five years ago, presumed dead. Emily, who had always loved games and puzzles. Emily, who had always been just a little bit too clever. The nervous man, the planted book, the hidden film – it was all a trap set by her own sister. Emily hadn't just disappeared; she had reinvented herself, and now she needed Sarah's unique skills, her quiet observation, to retrieve something that only a meticulous librarian would find. Sarah was not an accidental witness; she was the carefully chosen key in Emily's dangerous game. And now, Emily was coming for her and the lost page.
