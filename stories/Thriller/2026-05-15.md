# The Silent Mover

**Genre:** Thriller | **Date:** 2026-05-15

### Characters
Mike (a man who just moved into a new house)

### The Story
Mike loved clean lines. He liked things neat. So, when he moved into the old house, the first thing he saw was a problem: a fat, painted garden gnome by the front path. It had a red hat and a big, fake smile. Mike did not like it. He picked it up. It was heavier than it looked. He carried it to the back of the garden and tucked it deep into a thorny rose bush. "Out of sight," he muttered, "out of mind."

The next morning, Mike stepped onto his small porch for a coffee. And there it was. The gnome. Sitting right by the door, staring out at the street. Its silly smile seemed wider. Mike frowned. A prank? Maybe a neighbor. He picked it up again, walked it to the shed, and locked the door. "Good riddance," he thought.

Two days later, Mike came home from work. He unlocked his front door. And there it was. The gnome. Sitting on the small table in his hallway. Mike felt a cold shiver. The shed was locked. The house had been locked. How? He checked all the windows and doors. All shut tight. He walked around the house, looking at the bushes, the fences. Nothing. No signs of anyone. He picked up the gnome. He looked at its eyes. He put it in the kitchen pantry, behind some boxes, and shut the door tight. He felt a little jumpy.

The next morning, he made coffee. He opened the fridge for milk. The gnome was sitting on the top shelf, next to the jam. Mike yelled. He jumped back. His heart hammered. This wasn't funny anymore. This was scary. Someone was getting into his house. Someone was playing a game with him. He started to feel watched all the time. He kept the lights on at night. He bought new, strong locks for his doors. He even taped small pieces of string across windows, hoping to see if they broke.

Days turned into a week. Mike found the gnome in the living room, then in his spare bathroom. Each time, he felt sicker inside. He couldn't sleep well. Every creak of the old house made him jump. He started carrying a heavy flashlight when he walked from room to room after dark. He thought of calling the police, but what would he say? "A garden gnome keeps moving?" They'd think he was crazy.

One evening, he sat in his living room, tense, watching the windows. He had put the gnome in the attic. He was sure no one could get up there easily. He drifted off to sleep on the couch, the flashlight still in his hand. A loud crash woke him. He sat up, heart racing. He looked around the dark room. A faint shape was in the doorway to the kitchen. It was short. It had a round head. It had a silly red hat. The gnome. It had fallen over. Mike stared at it, frozen with fear. It felt like it was staring back. He knew he had to stop this. He had to know who was doing it.

He went to the store the next day and bought a small security camera. He set it up in his bedroom, facing his bed. He put the gnome on his bedside table, right where he had seen it that last terrifying night. He wanted to catch whoever put it there. He wanted proof. He fell asleep, tired and nervous.

### Conclusion
The next morning, Mike woke up. The gnome was still on his bedside table. He felt defeated. He checked the camera. He watched the video, fast-forwarding through the hours of him sleeping. Then, he saw it. Around three in the morning, Mike sat up in bed. His eyes were wide open, but blank. He slowly reached out, picked up the gnome, and held it. Then, he gently placed it back on the table, exactly where it was before. He lay down and went back to sleep. Mike watched himself do it, again and again. The blood drained from his face. It wasn't a prank. It wasn't a stranger. It was him. He was the silent mover. And he had been terrifying himself all along.
