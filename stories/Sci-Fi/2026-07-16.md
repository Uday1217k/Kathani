# The Pattern Keeper

**Genre:** Sci-Fi | **Date:** 2026-07-16

### Characters
Elara: A curious woman in her late twenties, observant and starting to feel uneasy.
Mika: Elara's friend, practical and less inclined to question daily life.

### The Story
Elara lived in a town called Haven. It was a good name. Everything was calm and neat. Houses lined up like good soldiers. Gardens bloomed in bright colors, always in season. Her job at the town library was quiet. Her days felt smooth, like a well-worn stone.

But lately, the smoothness felt too much. Too perfect.
She started to notice things. Small things.
Every morning, the bread she bought from the bakery had five tiny holes on top, never four, never six. Always five.
The news anchor on the evening broadcast always ended with the same words: "And so, a new day dawns." Every single night.
A small, red bird with a bright yellow patch on its chest would land on her window sill at exactly 7:03 AM. It would sing three short notes, then fly away. Every day.

Elara tried to tell her friend Mika about it during their lunch break. Mika just smiled, stirring her soup. "Elara, it's just how things are. Routine is nice, isn't it? Makes life easy."
But it didn't feel easy to Elara. It felt like a trap. Or a show.

One afternoon, Elara decided to break a pattern. Instead of walking the usual path home through the park, she took a detour down Elm Street. It was a street she rarely used. It wound around older houses, with bigger trees. She felt a small thrill of freedom.
But after ten minutes of walking, she saw something odd. The street ahead looked… familiar. Too familiar. She realized she was back near the park entrance, having somehow walked a big circle without meaning to. The feeling of being watched, of being guided, grew stronger.

That night, Elara couldn't sleep. The thought of the five holes in the bread, the red bird, the news anchor's words – it all buzzed in her head. She went to her window and looked up at the sky. It was dark, dotted with stars. Beautiful, but in a way that felt painted. Too still.
Then she saw it. A tiny shimmer. Like something briefly glinted and then faded. Not a star. It was higher. Far above the stars.

The next day, she skipped work. She knew what she had to do. The old clock tower stood at the edge of town, mostly forgotten. It was the tallest building, reaching high above the neat houses. She had never been up there.
The stairs were dusty and creaked under her feet. Each step made her heart beat faster. Finally, she reached the top, stepping out onto a narrow walkway.

A cold wind hit her face. She looked up.
What she saw made her stomach drop.

### Conclusion
Above her, covering their entire world, was a massive, transparent dome. It wasn't the sky. It was a ceiling. And on the surface of that dome, she could see shadows moving. Huge, indistinct shapes. They looked like... people. But impossibly big, like giants watching through a glass. Her world, her town, was just a small box.
Then, her eyes fell on a small, shiny plate fixed to the inside of the dome, just above her head. It was tarnished and old. She reached up and wiped away the dust with her sleeve.
The words etched onto the plate were simple, clear: "Project Earth-Seed. Human Colony 7-A. Est. 2147. Please do not feed the specimens."
Elara lowered her hand, her mind reeling. The patterns, the routines, the perfect sameness – it wasn't a flaw in her world. It was the care schedule. And she wasn't living in a town called Haven. She was living in a zoo.
