# The Memory Dust Collector

**Genre:** Sci-Fi | **Date:** 2026-05-28

### Characters
Elara: A young woman, quiet and observant. She cleans old records.
Mr. Finch: Her kind, older supervisor. He knows more than he lets on.

### The Story
Elara worked in the big, cold building they called The Vault. Her job was simple: clean up the old ‘memory dust.’ These were tiny bits of life data, records of moments, feelings, thoughts, all from people long gone or moved on. Her machine scanned dusty old cubes. If a cube held data that was too weak or too old to matter, she pressed the button to delete it. Then, space opened for new data. It was boring work.

She often found herself looking at the images that flashed on her screen before deletion. A sunny picnic. A dog chasing a ball. A quiet moment looking at rain. Sometimes, a feeling would wash over her, like she knew these moments. She would pause, almost ready to stop the deletion, but her job was clear. "Clear out the old to make room for the new," Mr. Finch always said.

One Tuesday, a particularly small, shiny cube came up. It was old, but the data inside was strong. It showed a little girl, maybe seven, laughing on a red swing set. The sun was bright. She could almost feel the wind on the girl's face, the joyful ache in her belly as she soared high. Elara felt a strange pull, a strong sense of knowing. This felt… familiar. Too familiar.

She looked closer at the tiny printed tag on the cube. The name was blurry, faded. She wiped it gently with her thumb. "Elara," it read. And below that, "Age 7."

Elara felt a chill. Her own name. Her own age. The red swing set felt like a dream she'd had, a place she'd been. But she didn't remember any red swing set from her childhood. She never had one. She had always lived in the city, in small apartments.

She took the cube to Mr. Finch's office. He was drinking his green tea. "Mr. Finch," she said, holding out the cube. "This… this has my name on it. And it's a child on a red swing. I don't remember this."

Mr. Finch smiled, a soft, tired smile. "Ah, yes," he said, taking the cube. He looked at it for a moment. "That's a very old one. From before the big data crunch."

"But it's *my* name. And it feels so real," Elara insisted, her voice tight.

"Yes, it does, doesn't it?" Mr. Finch said, his eyes crinkling. "You see, Elara, the Vault isn't just a place to store old memories. It's also where new ones come from. When people download their lives for safekeeping, sometimes we need to... rebuild. Reuse. The old, strong memories are like building blocks."

He tapped the cube gently. "You're a very good cleaner, Elara. You're efficient. You make excellent use of the space." He handed the cube back to her. "But you can keep this one. A little memento."

### Conclusion
Elara looked at the cube in her hand. The laughing girl on the red swing. The sun bright. The wind on her face. Her name. She walked back to her station, the cube feeling heavy. She placed it carefully on her desk, beside her terminal. Then, she looked at the list of memory dust cubes waiting for deletion. Another name popped up on the screen, faded but clear. "Elara, Age 12."
